# After The Yes — Delivery & The Five Products

Closing isn't the end of your job — it's the start of the relationship that funds
everything else. This lesson shows you what happens after "yes," and how one offer opens
the door to five products and a long, growing account. You don't sell the five products.
You sell the one offer, and you *route* into the products as the client's needs reveal
themselves.

## One offer, delivered by five products

The Brutal CRM Truth is what the client buys. Underneath, we deliver it through five
capabilities — and knowing which one the diagnostic points to is how you sound like an
expert, not a vendor:

1. **CRM Cleaning** — the core: merges, lifecycle fixes, dormants, bounces, opt-outs,
   re-enrichment. This is Execution.
2. **Outreach** — cold-outbound campaigns on the leads once the base is clean.
3. **Sales Reactivation (dialer)** — working the dormant pipeline the clean surfaced.
4. **ABM paid ads** — targeted paid on cleaned, enriched segments.
5. **ABM email** — targeted email on paying/known customers.

There is a **delivery dependency** baked into that order, and it's your roadmap for the
account: **clean data → sales data → ABM.** You can't run good ABM on a dirty base. So
CRM Cleaning always rides first — as prerequisite or bonus — and everything else becomes a
natural next step. That's not upselling; it's sequencing.

## The engagement, step by step

So the client can picture it (and you can set expectations on the call):

1. **Assessment** — they connect the CRM; a scorecard lands in ~24h; a full diagnostic in
   7–10 days with the one constraint named and a fix prescribed.
2. **Execution** — the clean itself, 7–14 days for a standard setup. The client spends
   under four hours approving batches; we do the rest.
3. **Continuity** — the base stays clean: ongoing validation, dedupe, drift alerts,
   refresh. This is where the account becomes recurring.

## Quarterly check-ins are where accounts grow

Every quarter you sit down to prove ROI (that's the Never-A-Cost mechanism). That same
meeting is your most natural upsell moment — because you're showing them results, not
pitching:

| Check-in | Natural next step |
|---|---|
| Q1 (post-clean) | Turn on **Continuity** if they haven't already. |
| Q2 | A diagnostic refresh + one targeted project. |
| Q3 | Self-serve reporting (business intelligence) if they keep asking "how do you know that?" |
| Q4 (year wrap) | A deeper done-with-you partnership if the fit is there. |

The pattern: **deliver a result → show it → let them ask for the next thing.** You earned
the right to the upsell by making the last one pay off.

## The rule that keeps you honest

Never walk in trying to sell all five products, and never sell a product the diagnostic
didn't call for. The client bought *the truth and a clean base*. You deliver exactly that,
brilliantly — and the five products reveal themselves as the obvious sequence to fix what
the truth uncovered. Sell the outcome, route to the product, earn the next yes.

## Key takeaways

- The client buys **one offer**; you deliver it through **five products** (Cleaning →
  Outreach → Reactivation → ABM ads → ABM email).
- The dependency **clean data → sales data → ABM** is your account roadmap — Cleaning
  always rides first.
- The engagement is **Assessment → Execution → Continuity**; Continuity makes it recurring.
- **Quarterly check-ins** are your best upsell moment — deliver, show, let them ask.
