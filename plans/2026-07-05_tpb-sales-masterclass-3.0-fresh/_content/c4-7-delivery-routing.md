# After The Yes: Delivery and the Five Products

Closing isn't the end of your job, it's the start of the relationship that funds everything
else. This lesson shows you what happens after "yes," and how one offer opens the door to
five products and a long, growing account. You don't sell the five products. You sell the
one offer and route into the products as the client's needs show up.

## One offer, delivered by five products

The Brutal CRM Truth is what the client buys. Underneath, we deliver it through five things
we can do, and knowing which one the diagnostic points to is how you sound like an expert
instead of a vendor:

1. CRM Cleaning, the core: merges, lifecycle fixes, dormants, bounces, opt-outs,
   re-enrichment. This is Execution.
2. Outreach, cold outbound on the leads once the base is clean.
3. Sales Reactivation, working the dormant pipeline the clean surfaced, with a dialer.
4. ABM paid ads, targeted paid on cleaned, enriched segments.
5. ABM email, targeted email on paying or known customers.

There's a delivery order baked into that list, and it's your roadmap for the account: clean
data, then sales data, then ABM. You can't run good ABM on a dirty base. So CRM Cleaning
always rides first, as a prerequisite or a bonus, and everything else becomes a natural next
step. That's not upselling, it's sequencing.

## The engagement, step by step

So the client can picture it, and so you can set expectations on the call:

1. Assessment. They connect the CRM, a scorecard lands in about a day, and a full diagnostic
   comes in 7 to 10 days with the one constraint named and a fix prescribed.
2. Execution. The clean itself, 7 to 14 days for a standard setup. The client spends under
   four hours approving batches, we do the rest.
3. Continuity. The base stays clean: ongoing validation, dedupe, drift alerts, refresh. This
   is where the account becomes recurring.

## Quarterly check-ins grow the account

Every quarter you sit down to prove ROI, which is the Never-A-Cost mechanism. That same
meeting is your most natural upsell moment, because you're showing results, not pitching.

| Check-in | Natural next step |
|---|---|
| Q1, post-clean | Turn on Continuity if they haven't already. |
| Q2 | A diagnostic refresh plus one targeted project. |
| Q3 | Self-serve reporting (business intelligence) if they keep asking "how do you know that?" |
| Q4, year wrap | A deeper done-with-you partnership if the fit is there. |

The pattern is simple. Deliver a result, show it, let them ask for the next thing. You
earned the right to the upsell by making the last one pay off.

## The rule that keeps you honest

Don't walk in trying to sell all five products, and don't sell a product the diagnostic
didn't call for. The client bought the truth and a clean base. Deliver exactly that, do it
well, and the five products show up as the obvious sequence to fix what the truth uncovered.
Sell the outcome, route to the product, earn the next yes.

## Recap

- The client buys one offer. You deliver it through five products: Cleaning, Outreach,
  Reactivation, ABM ads, ABM email.
- The order, clean data then sales data then ABM, is your account roadmap. Cleaning always
  rides first.
- The engagement is Assessment, then Execution, then Continuity. Continuity makes it
  recurring.
- Quarterly check-ins are your best upsell moment. Deliver, show, let them ask.
