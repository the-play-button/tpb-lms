# After The Yes: Delivery and the Five Products

The client bought one offer. You deliver it through five products, and knowing the order is
how you grow the account instead of selling it once.

The five: CRM cleaning (the core), outreach on the cleaned leads, sales reactivation on the
dormant pipeline, ABM paid ads, ABM email. The order is forced: clean data, then sales data,
then ABM. You can't run good ABM on a dirty base, so cleaning always goes first and the rest
follow. That's sequencing, not upselling.

## What happens after yes

So you can set expectations on the call:

1. Assessment: they connect the CRM, a scorecard lands in about a day, the full diagnostic in
   7 to 10 days with the one constraint named.
2. Execution: the clean, 7 to 14 days for a standard setup. They spend under four hours
   approving batches, we do the rest.
3. Continuity: the base stays clean over time. This is where the account becomes recurring.

## Grow the account at the check-ins

Every quarter you sit down to prove ROI. That same meeting is your best upsell moment,
because you're showing results, not pitching:

- Q1: turn on Continuity if it's not already on.
- Q2: a diagnostic refresh plus one targeted project.
- Q3: self-serve reporting if they keep asking "how do you know that?"
- Q4: a deeper partnership if the fit is there.

Deliver a result, show it, let them ask for the next thing. Never walk in selling all five,
and never sell a product the diagnostic didn't call for.
