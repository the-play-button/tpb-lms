# Your KPIs & Scorecard

You can't improve what you don't measure. This lesson gives you the numbers you own as a
rep, what good looks like early, and the weekly scorecard that tells you, honestly, whether
you're getting better.

## The metrics you own

Your job is a funnel. Track every stage, because a problem always lives in one specific
place, not in "sales" generally.

| Stage | What it measures | Where a problem shows up |
|---|---|---|
| Activity | Outbound touches or calls per day | Too few and pipeline dries up in 3 weeks |
| Conversations | Live conversations per week | Activity high but this low means your targeting or opener is off |
| Discovery booked | Qualified discovery calls booked per week | Conversations high but this low means you're not creating urgency |
| Assessments started | Free or served Assessments kicked off | Discovery high but this low means you're not landing the free-diagnostic next step |
| Execution closed | Paid engagements won | Assessments high but this low means the diagnostic isn't converting to the clean |

The point of the funnel: when your numbers dip, you don't panic about "selling," you look at
which stage dropped and fix that one thing.

## Early-stage targets

These are starting baselines, not forever numbers. They exist so you know if you're on track
before results have had time to show.

- Daily: a real activity number you hit every day, set with your coach. Consistency matters
  more than the exact figure.
- Weekly: a set number of live conversations and at least a handful of qualified discovery
  calls booked.
- The leading indicator that matters most early is Assessments started, because the free
  diagnostic is the wedge. If you're landing those, closes follow. If you're not, nothing
  downstream can work.

Your coach sets your specific targets in week one. The rule is measure daily, adjust weekly.

## The client-side scorecard

The offer has objective outcome metrics, and you should know them cold, because they're what
the guarantee is measured against:

- STRONG duplicates down 50%.
- Lifecycle accuracy up 30%.

Miss those on a paid engagement and Win-Your-Money-Back triggers a full refund. Hitting them
is what "we did the job" means. When a prospect asks "how will we know it worked?", these two
numbers are your answer.

## Keep your own CRM clean

You sell clean data. Keep your own pipeline spotless: every contact stage accurate, every
follow-up logged, no phantom deals. Two reasons. You'll close more because your own numbers
are trustworthy, and you'll sell the offer better because you live the pain and the payoff
yourself.

## Recap

- Own the funnel: activity, conversations, discovery booked, assessments started, execution
  closed. A dip lives in one stage. Fix that one.
- Early on, the leading indicator is Assessments started. The free diagnostic is the wedge.
- Know the client scorecard: STRONG duplicates down 50%, lifecycle accuracy up 30%. That's
  what the guarantee measures.
- Measure daily, adjust weekly, and keep your own CRM spotless.
