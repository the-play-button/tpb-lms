# Your KPIs & Scorecard

You can't improve what you don't measure. This lesson gives you the numbers you own as a
rep, what "good" looks like early, and the weekly scorecard that tells you — honestly —
whether you're getting better.

## The metrics you own

Your job breaks into a funnel. Track every stage, because a problem always lives in one
specific place, not "sales in general":

| Stage | What it measures | Where a problem shows up |
|---|---|---|
| **Activity** | Outbound touches / calls per day | Too few → pipeline dries up in 3 weeks |
| **Conversations** | Live conversations per week | Activity high but this low → your targeting or opener is off |
| **Discovery booked** | Qualified discovery calls booked / week | Conversations high but this low → you're not creating urgency |
| **Assessments started** | Free/served Assessments kicked off | Discovery high but this low → you're not landing the "free diagnostic" next step |
| **Execution closed** | Paid engagements won | Assessments high but this low → the diagnostic isn't converting to the clean |

The power of the funnel: when your numbers dip, you don't panic about "selling" — you look
at *which stage* dropped and fix that one thing.

## Early-stage targets (your first weeks)

These are starting baselines, not forever numbers. They exist so you know if you're on
track before results have had time to show:

- **Daily:** a consistent, real activity number you hit *every* day (set with your coach —
  consistency matters more than the exact figure).
- **Weekly:** a set number of live conversations and at least a handful of qualified
  discovery calls booked.
- **Leading indicator that matters most early:** **Assessments started.** Because the free
  diagnostic is the wedge — if you're landing those, closes follow. If you're not, nothing
  downstream can work.

Your coach sets your specific targets in week one. The rule: **measure daily, adjust
weekly.**

## The client-side scorecard (know the numbers you sell)

The offer itself has objective outcome metrics — and you should know them cold, because
they're what the guarantee is measured against:

- **Dedupe: STRONG duplicates down 50%.**
- **Lifecycle accuracy: up 30%.**

Miss those on a paid engagement and Win-Your-Money-Back triggers a full refund. Hitting
them is what "we did the job" means. When a prospect asks "how will we know it worked?" —
these two numbers are your answer.

## Practice your own hygiene (eat your own dog food)

You sell clean data. Keep *your* pipeline spotless: every contact stage accurate, every
follow-up logged, no phantom deals. Two reasons — you'll close more because your own
numbers are trustworthy, and you'll sell the offer better because you live the pain and the
payoff yourself.

## Key takeaways

- Own the **funnel**: activity → conversations → discovery booked → assessments started →
  execution closed. A dip lives in one stage — fix that one.
- Early on, the **leading indicator is Assessments started** — the free diagnostic is the
  wedge.
- Know the **client scorecard**: dedupe STRONG -50%, lifecycle accuracy +30% — that's what
  the guarantee measures.
- **Measure daily, adjust weekly**, and keep your own CRM spotless.
