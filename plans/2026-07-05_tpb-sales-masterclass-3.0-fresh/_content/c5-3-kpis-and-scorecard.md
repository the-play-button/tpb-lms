# Your KPIs & Scorecard

Your job is a funnel. Track every stage, because a problem always lives in one place, not in
"sales" generally:

| Stage | Measures | A dip here means |
|---|---|---|
| Activity | Outbound touches per day | Too few, pipeline dries up in 3 weeks |
| Conversations | Live conversations per week | Your targeting or opener is off |
| Discovery booked | Qualified calls booked per week | You're not creating urgency |
| Assessments started | Free or served diagnostics kicked off | You're not landing the free-diagnostic next step |
| Execution closed | Paid engagements won | The diagnostic isn't converting to the clean |

When a number dips, don't panic about "selling." Look at which stage dropped and fix that one
thing.

Early on, the number that matters most is Assessments started, because the free diagnostic is
the wedge. Land those and closes follow. Your coach sets your daily and weekly targets in
week one. Measure daily, adjust weekly.

## The numbers you sell on

The offer has two objective targets, and you should know them cold: duplicates down 50%,
lifecycle accuracy up 30%. Miss them on a paid engagement and money-back triggers a full
refund. Hit them and that's "we did the job." When a prospect asks "how will we know it
worked?", those two numbers are your answer.

Keep your own CRM spotless too. You'll close more when your numbers are trustworthy, and
you'll sell the offer better because you live the pain and the payoff yourself.
