# Discovery For This Offer

Every discovery call, you leave with four things. Get them and the price and close write
themselves:

1. The trigger. Which of the six live events is driving this.
2. The bucket. Standard, custom fields, or Custom stack. Sets the price.
3. The ARR. Above $5M or not. Sets the guarantee.
4. The constraint. The one business problem clean data unlocks for them.

## Open where they hurt

Don't interrogate. Start with their pain, in their words:

> "Before I tell you how we work, what made this land on your desk now? What's not happening
> that should be?"

Then be quiet and listen for a trigger. People hand you "our last campaign tanked" or "the
new VP wants the numbers to make sense" without being asked. Catch it and dig one layer:

> "When you say the campaign underperformed, was that the message or the list? Those are very
> different problems."

That quietly turns their problem into a data problem, which is what you fix.

## Route the bucket with two questions

Ask them like a person, not a form:

1. "Which CRM or CRMs are you running?" One of HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive is Standard.
   Multiple sources, or something custom like DECK or an ERP, is Custom stack.
2. "Are your fields basically the standard schema, or have you added a lot of custom ones?"
   Standard is Standard. Heavy custom is Standard with custom fields.

## Qualify the guarantee with one

> "Is your annual recurring revenue above $5M?"

Yes, they qualify for Never-A-Cost. No, they're on money-back terms only. Frame it well:
"Never-A-Cost is reserved for $5M+ ARR, where we can run the ROI mechanism cleanly. For your
size you're covered by our money-back terms instead."

## Name the constraint back

The offer's name is a promise: you tell them the one thing blocking growth. So say it,
specific and a little uncomfortable:

> "From what you're describing, I don't think your problem is more leads. You're sitting on a
> base that's about 30% dead weight, and it's dragging down every number you look at. Good
> news is that's exactly what we diagnose and fix, and for a standard HubSpot setup the
> diagnostic is free."
