# Discovery For This Offer

You learned a general discovery method in the Sales Conversation course. This lesson makes
it specific to The Brutal CRM Truth. By the end of a good discovery call you need four
things nailed down: the trigger, the bucket, the qualifier, and the constraint. Get those
four and the price and the close write themselves.

## The four things you leave with

1. The trigger, which of the six live events is driving this.
2. The bucket, Standard, Standard with custom fields, or Custom stack. This sets the price.
3. The qualifier, is ARR above $5M. This sets the guarantee.
4. The constraint, the one business problem clean data unlocks for them.

## Open by earning the right to the truth

Don't interrogate. Start where they hurt, in their words:

> "Before I tell you anything about how we work, what made this land on your desk now? What's
> the thing that's not happening that should be?"

Then be quiet and listen for a trigger. People hand you trigger #1 ("our last campaign
tanked") or #4 ("new VP wants the numbers to make sense") without being asked. Your job is
to catch it and dig one layer:

> "When you say the campaign underperformed, do you know if that was the message or the list?
> Because those are very different problems."

That question quietly turns their problem into a data problem, which is the thing you fix.

## Route the bucket with two questions

You need to price it right, and two client-readable questions get you there. Ask them
naturally, not like a form.

1. "Which CRM or CRMs are you running?"
   - One of HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive, that's Standard.
   - Multiple sources, or something custom like DECK, Dynamics, SAP CRM, or an ERP feeding a
     CRM, that's Custom stack.
2. "Are your contact fields basically the standard schema, or have you added a lot of custom
   fields and objects?"
   - Standard, that's Standard CRM.
   - Heavy custom, that's Standard CRM with custom fields.

That's it. Two answers route them to Free, Discounted, or Paid Assessment. When you hear
"well, we've got our ERP and HubSpot and honestly they don't really talk," that's a Custom
stack, and it's a bigger, better deal, not a harder one.

## Qualify the guarantee with one question

Ask it plainly. The client knows the answer, and it unlocks your strongest close:

> "Is your annual recurring revenue above $5M?"

- Yes. They qualify for Never-A-Cost on a $25k or larger engagement. You get to say "it pays
  for itself or we keep working, free."
- No. They're on Win-Your-Money-Back terms only, which you'll cover in objections and
  closing. Still sellable, just a different guarantee. Frame it well: "Never-A-Cost is
  reserved for $5M+ ARR, where we can run the ROI mechanism cleanly. For your size you're
  covered by our money-back terms instead."

ARR works as a proxy for everything that makes the value mechanism fire: an active sales
team, a CRM used daily, real marketing spend, deliverability that matters. And the client
can answer it honestly on the spot.

## Find their constraint

The offer's name is a promise. You tell them the one thing actually blocking growth. So
discovery has to surface a hypothesis for it. Listen for which of these they're living:

- Dormant pipeline nobody's working.
- Deliverability dragging every campaign down.
- Reporting they can't trust for the board or quarter close.
- Two systems and no source of truth.

Then name it back, specific and a little uncomfortable. That's the brutal part:

> "From what you're describing, I don't think your problem is more leads. I think you're
> sitting on a base that's about 30% dead weight, and it's dragging down every number you
> look at. Good news is that's exactly the thing we diagnose and fix, and for a standard
> HubSpot setup the diagnostic is free."

## Recap

- Leave every discovery with four things: trigger, bucket, qualifier, constraint.
- Open with their pain and quietly turn it into a data problem.
- Route the bucket with two questions, the guarantee with one (ARR above $5M?).
- Name the constraint back to them, specific and brutal, then offer the diagnostic (free for
  Standard CRM) as the obvious next step.
