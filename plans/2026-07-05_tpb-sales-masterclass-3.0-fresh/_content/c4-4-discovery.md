# Discovery For This Offer

You already learned a general discovery method in the Sales Conversation course. This
lesson makes it specific to The Brutal CRM Truth. By the end of a good discovery call you
must walk away with **four things nailed down**: the trigger, the bucket, the qualifier,
and the constraint. Get those four and the price and close write themselves.

## The four things you must leave with

1. **The trigger** — which of the six live events is driving this (see "Who We Sell To").
2. **The bucket** — Standard / Standard+custom fields / Custom stack (routes the price).
3. **The qualifier** — is ARR above $5M? (routes the guarantee).
4. **The constraint** — the one business problem clean data unlocks for *them*.

## Open by earning the right to the truth

Don't interrogate. Start where they hurt, in their words:

> *"Before I tell you anything about how we work — what made this land on your desk now?
> What's the thing that's not happening that should be?"*

Then shut up and listen for a trigger. People will hand you trigger #1 ("our last campaign
tanked") or #4 ("new VP wants the numbers to make sense") without being asked. Your job is
to recognize it and dig one layer:

> *"When you say the campaign underperformed — do you know if that was the message, or the
> list? Because those are very different problems."*

That question quietly reframes their problem as a **data** problem — which is what you fix.

## Route the bucket with exactly two questions

You need to price correctly, and you can do it with two client-readable questions. Ask
them naturally, not as a form:

1. **"Which CRM(s) are you running?"**
   - One of HubSpot / Salesforce / Pipedrive → **Standard.**
   - Multiple sources, or something custom (DECK, Dynamics, SAP CRM, an ERP feeding a
     CRM) → **Custom stack.**
2. **"Are your contact fields basically the standard schema, or have you added a lot of
   custom fields and objects?"**
   - Standard → **Standard CRM.**
   - Heavy custom → **Standard CRM with custom fields.**

That's it. Two answers route them to Free / Discounted / Paid Assessment automatically.
When you hear "well, we've got our ERP and HubSpot and honestly they don't really talk" —
that's a **Custom stack**, and it's a *bigger, better* deal, not a harder one.

## Qualify the guarantee with one question

Ask it plainly, because the client knows the answer and it unlocks your strongest close:

> **"Is your annual recurring revenue above $5M?"**

- **Yes** → they qualify for **Never-A-Cost** (on a $25k+ engagement). You get to say
  "it pays for itself or we keep working — free."
- **No** → they're on **Win-Your-Money-Back** terms only (covered in objections/closing).
  Still sellable, just a different guarantee. Frame it positively: *"Never-A-Cost is
  reserved for $5M+ ARR, where we can run the ROI mechanism cleanly. For your size, you're
  covered by our money-back terms instead."*

Why this question works: ARR is a clean proxy for everything that makes the value
mechanism fire — active sales team, daily-used CRM, real marketing spend, deliverability
that matters. And the client can answer it honestly on the spot.

## Find *their* constraint (this is the "Brutal Truth")

The offer's name is a promise: you tell them the **one thing** actually blocking growth.
So discovery has to surface a hypothesis for it. Listen for which of these they're living:

- Dormant pipeline nobody's working.
- Deliverability dragging every campaign down.
- Reporting they can't trust for board/quarter close.
- Two systems and no source of truth.

Then name it back, specific and a little uncomfortable — that's the "brutal" part:

> *"From what you're describing, I don't think your problem is more leads. I think you're
> sitting on a base that's ~30% dead weight, and it's dragging down every number you look
> at. The good news is that's exactly the thing we diagnose and fix — and for a standard
> HubSpot setup, the diagnostic is free."*

## Key takeaways

- Leave every discovery with **four things**: trigger, bucket, qualifier, constraint.
- Open with *their* pain and quietly reframe it as a **data** problem.
- Route the bucket with **two questions**, the guarantee with **one** (ARR > $5M?).
- Name the **constraint** back to them, specific and brutal — then offer the diagnostic
  (free for Standard CRM) as the obvious next step.
