# ⏰ Your Daily Rhythm

Once you're live, every day runs on the same rhythm. **The empty slots on today's calendar
are what fill tomorrow's**, so you never waste one. Here's how a day is built.

## Your day, hour by hour

Your day has two versions, and it's the **same person** running both. On a day with no calls
booked, you spend it hunting. On a normal day, you weave calls through the hunting. The empty
slots today are exactly what fill tomorrow's calendar.

**A day with no booked calls (all hunt):**

| Time | What you're doing |
|---|---|
| 6:00 | Review and prep, write up new notes |
| 6:30 to 9:00 | Priority list (3.5 hours of pure hunting) |
| 9:30 | Team training (30 min) |
| 10:00 to 2:30 | Priority list (4.5 hours) |
| 3:00 to 4:30 | Outbound (2 hours) |
| 5:00 to 6:00 | Priority list |
| 6:30 to 7:00 | End-of-day checklist |

**A normal day (calls booked):**

| Time | What you're doing |
|---|---|
| 6:00 | Review and prep, write up new notes |
| 6:30 to 7:00 | Priority list |
| 7:30 to 9:00 | **Close calls** |
| 9:30 | Team training |
| 10:00 to 11:00 | **Close calls** |
| 11:30 to 12:00 | Priority list |
| 12:30 to 1:00 | **Close calls** |
| 1:30 to 2:30 | Priority list |
| 3:00 to 4:30 | Outbound |
| 5:00 to 5:30 | **Close calls** |
| 6:00 | Priority list |
| 6:30 to 7:00 | End-of-day checklist |

Notice there's no idle time in either one. When you're not on a call, you're on your priority
list or doing outbound, filling tomorrow.

## You're always doing one of two things

Every hour, you're either **hunting** or **closing**. Both feed the same number.

- **Hunting:** getting people onto a call. Outbound, follow-ups, reactivating old leads.
- **Closing:** on the call, running your scripts, moving the deal.

**Rule:** when you're not on a call, you're booking one. Dead air between calls is the single
easiest thing to lose money on. Fill it.

## Work your leads in the right order

Not all leads are equal, and the order you work them decides how many close. Work them from
**most likely to buy** down.

| Priority | Who | Why now |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | A call just booked | They're hot right now. Call within minutes, not hours. |
| **2** | Today's scheduled calls | Committed time. Show up prepared. |
| **3** | Deals mid-conversation | Momentum dies if you go quiet. Keep it moving. |
| **4** | No-shows and missed calls | Chase the same day, while they still remember booking. |
| **5** | Cold and old leads | Your outbound block. Volume game, run it last. |

**A fresh booking is the hottest lead you'll ever have.** The person is sitting there, phone
in hand, thinking about you. Every minute you wait, that cools. Call them first.

## End-of-day close-out

Before you log off, run the checklist. This is what makes tomorrow start fast instead of
starting with cleanup.

- [ ] Every call logged with notes
- [ ] Every open deal updated to its real next step
- [ ] Every no-show chased or scheduled
- [ ] Tomorrow's calls confirmed
- [ ] Your first outbound block already planned

Do this every day and you walk in tomorrow knowing exactly who to call and what to say. Skip
it and you spend your best hour cleaning up instead of selling.
