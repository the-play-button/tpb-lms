# How To Use This Course

Cold outbound is a core skill for every TPB rep: reaching a stranger and earning a reply.
This course teaches it in full, from Nick Saraev, who did $15M in outbound sales. The lessons
that follow are his actual course video, split into seven parts. You watch the video in each
lesson, and the notes underneath are there so you can find a point again later without
scrubbing through the whole thing.

## What you're about to watch

Seven parts, in order, take you from zero to competent:

1. The psychology of trust, the seven levers that make strangers say yes.
2. The three components of outbound that actually works.
3. The copywriting framework, a cold email taken apart line by line.
4. Offers, what to actually put in front of people.
5. Roasting real emails, ten live rewrites, so you see the fixes, not just the rules.
6. Platforms, subject lines, follow-ups: email, LinkedIn, DMs, and the iteration loop.
7. AI in copywriting, and where the edges are.

## How to work through it

Watch in order. Each part builds on the last, so don't skip ahead to offers before you've
got the trust stuff down. Write as you watch. After each part, rewrite one of your own cold
emails using what you just learned. Reading it changes nothing, using it changes everything.
And steal the phrasings. When a line lands for you, save it. You're building a swipe file
you'll use for years.

## How this connects to what we sell

The outbound skill works for any offer, but you'll point it at ours, The Brutal CRM Truth,
covered in the Offer course. As you go through Nick's framework, keep our buyer in mind: the
VP Sales or VP Marketing sitting on a dying pipeline, with one of six live triggers. The best
cold email you'll ever write names their trigger and offers their outcome. This course
teaches you how to write it.

One thing to carry in from our own philosophy: the human voice is sacred. Good outbound
never sounds like a spam cannon. It sounds like a specific person who did their homework and
said something true. Everything Nick teaches serves that.
