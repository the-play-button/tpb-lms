# Pricing & Closing

You've diagnosed the constraint, anchored the value, and handled the objections. Now you put
a price together and close. Good news: pricing here is a configurator, not a negotiation, and
the guarantees do most of the closing for you.

## Price is a configuration, not a number you invent

You never pull a price out of thin air. You build it from four pieces. Learn the shape so you
can put a quote together live on a call.

First, modules, what they're buying:

| Module | Start price |
|---|---|
| Assessment | Free (Standard), $5k to $15k (custom fields), $5k to $80k (Custom stack, by tier) |
| Execution | $18k (DIY), $35k (DWY group), $50k (DWY 1:1), $70k (DFY) |
| Continuity | $1k to $5k per month by contact volume |

Second, tier, meaning who does the work, not what's delivered: DIY, then DWY group, then DWY
1:1, then DFY. More done-for-you means a higher price and less client effort. DFY is the
reference.

Third, options, add-ons priced separately when the stack needs them:

| Option | Add-on |
|---|---|
| Bridge integration (like DECK to HubSpot) | $10k to $40k |
| Multi-CRM unification (post-merger) | $15k to $50k |
| Custom segmentation modeling | $5k to $25k |
| Sender warm-up plan | $3k to $10k |

Two worked examples you can use as templates:

> Standard HubSpot, 8k contacts, weak sales. Assessment free, Execution DFY $70k, Continuity
> $2k a month. That's $70k upfront plus $24k year-one recurring.
>
> Wonder Grip Europe, a Custom stack. Assessment DWY 1:1 $30k plus Bridge $25k, Execution DFY
> $70k plus Custom segmentation $15k, Continuity $5k a month. That's $140k upfront plus $60k
> year-one recurring.

## Stack the guarantees before you name the price

Before the number lands, put the risk on the table so "$70k" arrives into a context where
saying no feels off. Walk all four, headline first:

1. Never-A-Cost (ARR above $5M): "It pays for itself or we work free until it does."
2. Your-Data-Stays-Yours: "Never sold, shared, or used to train AI. Yours, period."
3. Zero-Loss Backup: "We touch a record without your approval, we restore it and credit you
   $5,000."
4. 30-Day One-Click Rollback: "Changed your mind? One click, full reversal, 30 days, no
   questions."

Plus Win-Your-Money-Back: miss the objective scorecard targets and it's a 100% cash refund.
Once a prospect has heard all of that, the price is the least scary thing in the room.

## Use real scarcity and their urgency

Scarcity, real and capacity-based: "We onboard 4 companies a month per success manager. This
quarter has 3 spots left." It's true, the human approval step caps throughput. Never fake
it.

Urgency, theirs not yours: anchor to their live trigger, the campaign, the quarter close,
the migration. Never a TPB countdown timer.

## The close

With the constraint named, the value anchored, the guarantees stacked, and a spot on the
line, the close is quiet and assumptive:

> "So here's what I'd do. We run the diagnostic, free since you're on HubSpot, and you see
> your real constraint on your actual data in about a week. If it's what I think it is, we go
> straight into a done-for-you clean at $70k, fully guaranteed to pay for itself. We've got 3
> onboarding spots this quarter and your campaign is in three weeks, so I'd grab one now and
> we'll have you clean before it sends. Want me to reserve it?"

Then stop talking. If the buyer is hesitant, fall back to the smallest yes, the free
Assessment, because once they see their own brutal truth on their own data, the Execution
sells itself.

## Recap

- Price is a configurator: modules, tier, options. Build it live, don't invent numbers.
- Stack the four guarantees plus WYMB before naming the price. The risk reversal is your
  close.
- Use real scarcity (4 a month per CSM) and their urgency (the live trigger).
- Close assumptively toward the free Assessment first. The diagnostic sells the clean.
