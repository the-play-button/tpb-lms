# Pricing & Closing

You've diagnosed the constraint, anchored the value, and dissolved the objections. Now you
configure a price and close. The good news: pricing here is a **configurator, not a
negotiation**, and the guarantees do most of the closing for you.

## Price is a configuration, not a number you invent

You never pull a price from thin air. You assemble it from four dimensions. Learn the shape
so you can build a quote live on a call:

**1. Modules** — what they're buying:

| Module | Start price |
|---|---|
| Assessment | Free (Standard) · $5–15k (custom fields) · $5–80k (Custom stack, by tier) |
| Execution | $18k (DIY) · $35k (DWY group) · $50k (DWY 1:1) · **$70k (DFY)** |
| Continuity | $1–5k / month by contact volume |

**2. Tier** — *who does the work* (not what's delivered): DIY → DWY group → DWY 1:1 → DFY.
More done-for-you = higher price and lower client effort. DFY is the reference.

**3. Options** — add-ons, priced separately when the stack needs them:

| Option | Add-on |
|---|---|
| Bridge integration (e.g. DECK ↔ HubSpot) | $10–40k |
| Multi-CRM unification (post-merger) | $15–50k |
| Custom segmentation modeling | $5–25k |
| Sender warm-up plan | $3–10k |

**Two worked configurations** (use these as templates):

> **Standard HubSpot, 8k contacts, weak sales:** Assessment free → Execution DFY $70k →
> Continuity $2k/mo = **$70k upfront + $24k year-one recurring.**
>
> **Wonder Grip Europe (Custom stack):** Assessment DWY 1:1 $30k + Bridge $25k → Execution
> DFY $70k + Custom segmentation $15k → Continuity $5k/mo = **$140k upfront + $60k
> year-one recurring.**

## Stack the guarantees before you name the price

Before the number lands, put the risk-reversal on the table so "$70k" arrives into a
context where saying no feels irrational. Walk all four, headline first:

1. **Never-A-Cost** (ARR > $5M) — *"it pays for itself or we work free until it does."*
2. **Your-Data-Stays-Yours** — *"never sold, shared, or used to train AI. Yours, period."*
3. **Zero-Loss Backup** — *"we touch a record without your approval, we restore it and
   credit you $5,000."*
4. **30-Day One-Click Rollback** — *"changed your mind? One click, full reversal, 30 days,
   no questions."*

Plus **Win-Your-Money-Back**: miss the objective scorecard targets and it's a 100% cash
refund. When a prospect has heard all of that, the price is the least scary thing in the
room.

## Use real scarcity and their urgency

- **Scarcity (real, capacity-based):** *"We onboard 4 companies per month per success
  manager. This quarter has 3 spots left."* It's true — the human approval step caps
  throughput. Never fake it.
- **Urgency (theirs, not yours):** anchor to their live trigger — the campaign, the
  quarter close, the migration. Never a TPB countdown timer.

## The close

With the constraint named, value anchored, guarantees stacked, and a spot on the line, the
close is quiet and assumptive:

> *"So here's what I'd do: we run the diagnostic — free, since you're on HubSpot — you see
> your real constraint on your actual data in about a week. If it's what I think it is, we
> go straight into a done-for-you clean at $70k, fully guaranteed to pay for itself. We've
> got 3 onboarding spots this quarter and your campaign is in three weeks, so I'd grab one
> now and we'll have you clean before it sends. Want me to reserve it?"*

Then stop talking. For a hesitant buyer, fall back to the smallest yes — the free
Assessment — because once they see their own brutal truth on their own data, the Execution
sells itself.

## Key takeaways

- Price is a **configurator**: modules × tier × options. Build it live; don't invent
  numbers.
- **Stack the four guarantees + WYMB before naming the price** — the risk-reversal is your
  close.
- Use **real scarcity** (4/month per CSM) and **their** urgency (the live trigger).
- Close assumptively toward the **free Assessment** first — the diagnostic sells the clean.
