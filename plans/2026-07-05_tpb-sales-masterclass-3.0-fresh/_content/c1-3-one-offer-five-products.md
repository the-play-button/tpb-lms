# One Offer, Five Products

This is the mental model that keeps you focused and keeps the company from scattering. Get
it now and everything in the Offer course will make sense.

## We sell one offer, on purpose

We're launching a business. The temptation is always to sell everything at once, and that's
a mistake we're deliberately not making. At launch we sell one offer: we wake up your
dormant pipeline. A clean, trustworthy base that produces again. You'll go deep on it in the
Offer course.

Why one? Because focus wins launches. Hormozi calls it the beachhead. Pick one thing, for
one kind of buyer, and get undeniably good at it before you widen. A rep selling one sharp
offer beats a rep juggling five, every time.

## The five products sit underneath the offer

Behind that one offer are five things we can do to deliver it:

1. CRM Cleaning, the foundation.
2. Outreach, cold outbound on the cleaned leads.
3. Sales Reactivation, working the dormant pipeline with a dialer.
4. ABM paid ads, targeted paid on cleaned segments.
5. ABM email, targeted email on known customers.

These are not five offers to sell. They're how we fulfill the one promise, and which ones a
client gets depends on what they actually need. The client buys the outcome. You route to
the right product.

## Product vs. offer

Keep these two words separate in your head. Mixing them up is what makes reps sound
scattered.

A product is a thing we can do, like cleaning, outreach, or a dialer campaign. An offer is a
promise of an outcome that a specific buyer says yes to.

Hormozi's line is "sell the vacation, not the plane flight." The client doesn't want the
mechanics, which are the products. They want the destination, which is the outcome. You lead
with the vacation. The products are how we get them there, and that's your call, not their
decision.

## The delivery dependency

The five products aren't interchangeable. There's an order that reality forces on you:

clean data, then sales data, then ABM.

You can't run good outreach or ABM on a dirty base. So CRM Cleaning always comes first,
either as a prerequisite or a bonus, and the rest follow once the foundation is solid. That
one dependency is why we lead with cleaning, and it's why every account has a built-in path
to grow.

## Recap

- At launch we sell one offer, the beachhead. Focus beats a menu.
- Five products deliver that offer. They're how we fulfill it, not five things to pitch.
- A product is what we can do. An offer is an outcome a buyer says yes to. Sell the vacation,
  not the flight.
- The order is clean data, then sales data, then ABM. Cleaning always rides first, and it
  gives every account a roadmap to grow.
