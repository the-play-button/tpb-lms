# One Offer, Five Products

This is the mental model that keeps you focused and keeps the company from scattering. Get
it now and everything in the Offer course will click.

## We sell one offer — on purpose

We are launching a business. The temptation is always to sell everything at once. That's a
mistake, and we're deliberately not making it. At launch, we sell **one offer**: *"we wake
up your dormant pipeline"* — a clean, trustworthy base that produces again. (You'll go deep
on it in the Offer course.)

Why one? Because focus wins launches. Hormozi calls it the beachhead: pick one thing, for
one kind of buyer, and become undeniably good at it before you widen. A rep selling one
sharp offer beats a rep juggling five every time.

## The five products sit *underneath* the offer

Behind that one offer are five capabilities that deliver it:

1. **CRM Cleaning** — the foundation.
2. **Outreach** — cold outbound on the cleaned leads.
3. **Sales Reactivation (dialer)** — working the dormant pipeline.
4. **ABM paid ads** — targeted paid on cleaned segments.
5. **ABM email** — targeted email on known customers.

These are **not** five offers to sell. They're internal delivery knowledge — the ways we
fulfill the one promise, chosen based on what the client actually needs. The client buys
the outcome; we route to the right product.

## Product vs. offer — the distinction that matters

Keep these two words separate in your head, because mixing them up is what makes reps
sound scattered:

- A **product** is a thing we can do (cleaning, outreach, a dialer campaign).
- An **offer** is a *promise of an outcome* that a specific buyer says yes to.

Hormozi's line: **"sell the vacation, not the plane flight."** The client doesn't want the
mechanics (the products); they want the destination (the outcome). You lead with the
vacation. The products are how we get them there — your job, not their decision.

## The delivery dependency (your account roadmap)

The five products aren't interchangeable — there's an order forced by reality:

> **Clean data → sales data → ABM.**

You can't run good outreach or ABM on a dirty base. So **CRM Cleaning always comes first**
— as prerequisite or bonus — and the rest follow as natural next steps once the foundation
is solid. That single dependency is why we lead with cleaning and why every account has a
built-in path to grow.

## Key takeaways

- At launch we sell **one offer** — the beachhead. Focus beats a menu.
- **Five products** deliver that offer; they're internal delivery knowledge, not five
  things to pitch.
- A **product** is what we can do; an **offer** is an outcome a buyer says yes to. Sell the
  vacation, not the flight.
- The dependency **clean data → sales data → ABM** means cleaning always rides first — and
  gives every account a roadmap to grow.
