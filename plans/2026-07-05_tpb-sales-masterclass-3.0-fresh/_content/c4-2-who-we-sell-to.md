# Who We Sell To — ICP, Triggers, and Buckets

A great offer sold to the wrong person still fails. This lesson makes you fast at one
thing: **in the first two minutes of any conversation, know whether this is a real buyer
and which version of the offer they need.**

## The avatar (who qualifies)

> **B2B, US, mid-market — 50+ employees, 5,000+ contacts** in their primary CRM
> (HubSpot / Salesforce / Pipedrive, or a custom multi-source stack), where the **VP
> Sales / VP Marketing / COO / CEO** has at least one live trigger in the last 30–60 days.

Three hard filters do most of the work:

- **50+ employees.** Below that, there's no sales ops pain worth our price.
- **5,000+ CRM contacts.** Below that, the base is too small for a diagnostic to matter.
- **US, B2B.** (Scope is US/CCPA for now. Not B2C.)

## The exclusion list (disqualify fast — it's a favor to both of you)

- **Not** B2C.
- **Not** SMBs under 50 employees — dead zone, walk away.
- **Not** companies under 5,000 contacts — insufficient base.
- **Not** outside the US.
- No upper cap on employee count — bigger is fine.

Disqualifying early is a skill, not a failure. A clean "you're not a fit, here's who is"
builds more trust than forcing a bad deal.

## The six triggers (why *now*)

Nobody buys "someday." They buy when something just happened. Your job in discovery is to
find which of these six is live — because the trigger is your urgency, and you don't have
to manufacture it. Learn these cold:

1. **Email campaign with delivery rate below 70%** — deliverability is bleeding.
2. **Outbound launch paused by RevOps for data quality** — they're blocked and know it.
3. **Post-merger CRM reconciliation in progress** — two systems, no source of truth.
4. **New VP Sales or VP Marketing in their first 60 days** — needs quick wins and trust
   in the data.
5. **Pre-audit CCPA / privacy compliance check** — legal pressure on the contact base.
6. **Multi-source CRM stack with no single source of truth** (e.g. industrial ERP + CRM
   bridge) — this is the WGE archetype.

**How to use a trigger in copy or on a call** — anchor to *their* deadline, not a fake
one:

> *"Before your next campaign goes out — a CRM with 30%+ duplicates or stale lifecycle
> hits delivery hard. We clean it and have you ready in 7 days for a standard HubSpot /
> Salesforce / Pipedrive setup."*

No trigger, no urgency. If you can't find one of the six, you're either talking to the
wrong person or it's not their moment — note it and follow up when it becomes their moment.

## The three buckets (which version of the offer they get)

The offer is the same for everyone; the **entry** changes based on how complex their CRM
stack is. You'll route this in discovery with two questions (next lesson). Learn the three
buckets now:

| Bucket (this is client-facing language) | What it looks like | How the Assessment is priced |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard CRM** | HubSpot / Salesforce / Pipedrive, single source, standard schema | **Free** — the diagnostic is our lead magnet, no strings |
| **Standard CRM with custom fields** | Same, but significant custom fields/objects to map | **Discounted** ($5–15k) |
| **Custom CRM stack** | Multi-source (e.g. DECK + HubSpot), custom bridge, post-merger, custom segmentation, multi-org | **Paid** ($20–80k), because it takes real integration work |

**Why "free" for Standard CRM is a strength, not a discount you're apologizing for:** a
free diagnostic is the strongest trust signal you can send. It says *"we're so sure we'll
find something worth paying to fix that we'll show you first, for nothing."* You're a
service provider proving value up front — not a data buyer trading them a report for their
list.

**The canonical Custom CRM stack example — Wonder Grip Europe:** DECK (industrial ERP) +
a required HubSpot bridge + market segmentation modeled custom in DECK. That's a paid
Assessment with bridge integration and custom segmentation switched on. Whenever you hear
"we have our ERP and our CRM and they don't really talk," you're in bucket three.

## Key takeaways

- Qualify hard on three filters: **50+ employees, 5,000+ contacts, US B2B.**
- Disqualify fast and cleanly — it builds trust and saves everyone time.
- Find the **live trigger** (one of six). The trigger *is* your urgency — never fake it.
- Route to one of **three buckets** — the offer is the same, only the entry price of the
  Assessment changes with stack complexity.
