# Who We Sell To: ICP, Triggers, Buckets

A great offer sold to the wrong person still fails. This lesson makes you fast at one thing:
in the first two minutes of a conversation, knowing whether this is a real buyer and which
version of the offer they need.

## The avatar

Here's who qualifies. B2B, US, mid-market. 50 or more employees, 5,000 or more contacts in
their primary CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, or a custom multi-source stack), where
the VP Sales, VP Marketing, COO, or CEO has a live trigger in the last 30 to 60 days.

Three hard filters do most of the work:

- 50+ employees. Below that there's no sales ops pain worth our price.
- 5,000+ CRM contacts. Below that the base is too small for a diagnostic to matter.
- US, B2B. Scope is US and CCPA for now. Not B2C.

## The exclusion list

Disqualify fast. It's a favor to both of you.

- Not B2C.
- Not SMBs under 50 employees. Dead zone, walk away.
- Not companies under 5,000 contacts. Not enough base.
- Not outside the US.
- No upper cap on employee count. Bigger is fine.

Disqualifying early is a skill, not a failure. A clean "you're not a fit, here's who is"
builds more trust than forcing a bad deal.

## The six triggers

Nobody buys "someday." They buy when something just happened. Your job in discovery is to
find which of these six is live, because the trigger is your urgency and you don't have to
manufacture it. Learn them cold.

1. Email campaign delivering below 70%. Deliverability is bleeding.
2. Outbound launch paused by RevOps for data quality. They're blocked and they know it.
3. Post-merger CRM reconciliation in progress. Two systems, no source of truth.
4. New VP Sales or VP Marketing in their first 60 days. Needs quick wins and data they can
   trust.
5. Pre-audit CCPA or privacy check. Legal pressure on the contact base.
6. Multi-source CRM stack with no single source of truth, like an industrial ERP plus a CRM
   bridge. This is the WGE archetype.

To use a trigger in copy or on a call, anchor to their deadline, not a fake one:

> "Before your next campaign goes out. A CRM with 30% duplicates or stale lifecycle hits
> delivery hard. We clean it and have you ready in 7 days for a standard HubSpot,
> Salesforce, or Pipedrive setup."

No trigger, no urgency. If you can't find one of the six, you're either talking to the wrong
person or it's not their moment. Note it and follow up when it becomes their moment.

## The three buckets

The offer is the same for everyone. What changes is the entry, based on how complex their
CRM stack is. You'll route this in discovery with two questions (next lesson). Learn the
three buckets now.

| Bucket (this is client-facing language) | What it looks like | How the Assessment is priced |
|---|---|---|
| Standard CRM | HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive, single source, standard schema | Free. The diagnostic is our lead magnet, no strings |
| Standard CRM with custom fields | Same, but a lot of custom fields or objects to map | Discounted, $5k to $15k |
| Custom CRM stack | Multi-source (like DECK plus HubSpot), custom bridge, post-merger, custom segmentation, multi-org | Paid, $20k to $80k, because it takes real integration work |

A quick note on "free" for Standard CRM. Don't apologize for it. A free diagnostic is the
strongest trust signal you can send. It says we're so sure we'll find something worth paying
to fix that we'll show you first, for nothing. You're proving value up front, not trading a
report for their list.

The canonical Custom CRM stack example is Wonder Grip Europe: DECK (an industrial ERP) plus
a required HubSpot bridge plus market segmentation modeled inside DECK. That's a paid
Assessment with bridge integration and custom segmentation turned on. Whenever you hear "we
have our ERP and our CRM and they don't really talk," you're in bucket three.

## Recap

- Qualify hard on three filters: 50+ employees, 5,000+ contacts, US B2B.
- Disqualify fast and cleanly. It builds trust and saves everyone time.
- Find the live trigger, one of six. The trigger is your urgency, so never fake it.
- Route to one of three buckets. The offer is the same. Only the entry price of the
  Assessment changes with stack complexity.
