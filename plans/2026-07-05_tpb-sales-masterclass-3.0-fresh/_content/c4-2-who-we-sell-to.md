# 🧭 Who We Sell To: ICP, Triggers, Buckets

**Here's who qualifies.** B2B, US, mid-market. Three hard filters do most of the work:

- **50+ employees.** Below that there's no sales ops pain worth our price.
- **5,000+ CRM contacts.** Below that the base is too small for a diagnostic to matter.
- **US and B2B.** Scope is US and CCPA for now.

Miss any of those and you disqualify, cleanly: "you're not a fit, here's who is." That builds
more trust than forcing a bad deal. Also out: SMBs under 50, anyone under 5,000 contacts,
anyone outside the US, and B2C. No upper cap on size, bigger is fine.

## The six triggers: why now

Nobody buys "someday." They buy when something just happened. **Find which of these is live
and you don't have to manufacture urgency.** Learn them cold:

1. **Email delivering below 70%.** Deliverability is bleeding.
2. **Outbound launch paused by RevOps** for data quality. They're blocked and they know it.
3. **Post-merger CRM reconciliation.** Two systems, no source of truth.
4. **New VP Sales or Marketing, first 60 days.** Needs quick wins and data they trust.
5. **Pre-audit CCPA or privacy check.** Legal pressure on the contact base.
6. **Multi-source stack with no source of truth,** like an ERP plus a CRM bridge. This is the
   WGE case.

**Anchor to their deadline, not a fake one:**

> "Before your next campaign goes out. A CRM with 30% duplicates or stale lifecycle hits
> delivery hard. We clean it and have you ready in 7 days for a standard HubSpot, Salesforce,
> or Pipedrive setup."

No trigger, no urgency. If you can't find one, it's the wrong person or the wrong moment.

## The three buckets: which entry they get

The offer is the same for everyone. Only the **entry price of the diagnostic** changes with
how messy their stack is. Route it with two questions in discovery:

- **Standard CRM** (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, single source): diagnostic is **free.**
- **Standard CRM with custom fields:** diagnostic **$5k to $15k.**
- **Custom CRM stack** (multi-source, a bridge, post-merger, multi-org): diagnostic **$20k to
  $80k,** because it's real integration work.

**Don't apologize for "free."** A free diagnostic is the strongest trust signal you can send:
we're so sure we'll find something worth fixing that we'll show you first, for nothing. When
you hear "we've got our ERP and our CRM and they don't really talk," that's a **Custom
stack,** and it's a bigger, better deal, not a harder one.
