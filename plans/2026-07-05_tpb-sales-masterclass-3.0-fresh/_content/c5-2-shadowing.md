# The Shadowing Protocol

Before you run discovery solo, you shadow real calls in three phases. Don't advance until you
hit the bar on the one you're in.

Phase 1, observe (5+ calls). Muted, listening, filling the sheet below live. Five minutes
after each call on what moved the deal and what stalled it.

Phase 2, co-pilot (5+ calls). You handle one slice, usually the opening and the trigger dig,
or the value anchor. The rep runs the rest and takes over if you stall.

Phase 3, lead with a net (3+ calls). You run the whole call, the rep jumps in only if it's
going off a cliff. If they never had to on two of three, you're cleared to run solo.

## Fill this on every call

- Trigger: which surfaced, and how the rep dug it out.
- Bucket: how the rep routed it.
- ARR: did the rep ask, and what it unlocked.
- Constraint: how the rep named the brutal truth back.
- Objections: which came up, and which guarantee the rep used.
- The turn: the moment the prospect leaned in, and what caused it.
- The close: the next step, and how it was asked for.
- One thing to steal.

## Watch the small stuff

The mechanics are easy to see. The craft is in the details: silence after a question, not
flinching when $70k lands, turning "we do inbound" into a bridge instead of a fight, naming
the constraint with a bit of discomfort but warmly.

Once you're cleared, newer reps shadow you. Teaching keeps you sharp.
