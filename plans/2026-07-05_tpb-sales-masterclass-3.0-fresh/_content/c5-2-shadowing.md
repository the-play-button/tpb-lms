# The Shadowing Protocol

Roleplay makes you competent. Watching real calls makes you fluent. Before you run
discovery solo, you'll shadow real conversations — first as an observer, then as a
co-pilot. This lesson is the exact protocol so shadowing is deliberate practice, not
just "sitting in."

## The three phases

You move through three phases. You don't advance until you've hit the bar on the current
one.

**Phase 1 — Observe (5+ calls).** You're muted, camera optional, listening. Your only job
is to fill the observation sheet (below) live. After each call, you and the rep spend five
minutes on: *what moved the deal, what stalled it, what you'd have done differently.*

**Phase 2 — Co-pilot (5+ calls).** You handle one defined slice — usually the opening (the
"what made this land on your desk now?" and the trigger dig) or the value anchor. The rep
runs the rest and takes over if you stall. Debrief the same way.

**Phase 3 — Lead with a net (3+ calls).** You run the whole call; the rep is muted, ready
to jump in only if it's going off a cliff. If they never had to jump in on two of three,
you're cleared to run solo.

## The observation sheet (fill it live, every call)

For every call you shadow, capture:

- **Trigger:** which of the six surfaced? How did the rep dig it out?
- **Bucket:** how did the rep route it (the two questions)?
- **Qualifier:** did the rep ask ARR? What did it unlock?
- **Constraint:** how did the rep name the "brutal truth" back to them?
- **Objections:** which came up, which guarantee did the rep point to?
- **The turn:** the exact moment the prospect went from guarded to leaning in — what
  caused it?
- **The close:** what was the next step, and how was it asked for?
- **One thing I'd steal.** One phrasing or move you're going to use.

## What to watch for (the subtle stuff)

The mechanics are easy to see. The craft is in the small things — watch for these:

- **Silence after a question.** Good reps ask, then shut up. Count the seconds.
- **Not flinching on price.** Watch the rep's face and pace when $70k lands.
- **Reframing without arguing.** How they turn "we do inbound" or "we can do it internally"
  into a bridge instead of a fight.
- **Naming the constraint with a little discomfort.** The "brutal" delivered warmly.

## After you're cleared

Shadowing never fully ends. Once you're solo, you flip roles: newer reps shadow *you*, and
teaching forces you to sharpen. And you keep a running list of the best moves you steal from
teammates — that list becomes your personal playbook.

## Key takeaways

- Three phases: **observe → co-pilot → lead with a net.** Don't advance until you hit the
  bar.
- Fill the **observation sheet** live on every call — trigger, bucket, qualifier,
  constraint, objections, the turn, the close, one thing to steal.
- Watch the **craft**: silence, not flinching, reframing, the brutal-but-warm constraint.
- Cleared reps become shadowees — teaching keeps you sharp.
