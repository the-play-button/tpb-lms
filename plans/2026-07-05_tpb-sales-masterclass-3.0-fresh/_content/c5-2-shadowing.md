# The Shadowing Protocol

Roleplay makes you competent. Watching real calls makes you fluent. Before you run discovery
on your own, you'll shadow real conversations, first as an observer, then as a co-pilot. This
lesson is the protocol so shadowing is real practice, not just sitting in.

## The three phases

You move through three phases, and you don't advance until you've hit the bar on the current
one.

Phase 1, observe, 5 or more calls. You're muted, camera optional, listening. Your only job
is to fill in the observation sheet below, live. After each call you and the rep spend five
minutes on what moved the deal, what stalled it, and what you'd have done differently.

Phase 2, co-pilot, 5 or more calls. You handle one defined slice, usually the opening (the
"what made this land on your desk now?" and the trigger dig) or the value anchor. The rep
runs the rest and takes over if you stall. Debrief the same way.

Phase 3, lead with a net, 3 or more calls. You run the whole call, the rep is muted and ready
to jump in only if it's going off a cliff. If they never had to jump in on two of three,
you're cleared to run solo.

## The observation sheet

Fill it in live, on every call:

- Trigger. Which of the six surfaced? How did the rep dig it out?
- Bucket. How did the rep route it, with the two questions?
- Qualifier. Did the rep ask ARR? What did it unlock?
- Constraint. How did the rep name the brutal truth back to them?
- Objections. Which came up, and which guarantee did the rep point to?
- The turn. The exact moment the prospect went from guarded to leaning in. What caused it?
- The close. What was the next step, and how was it asked for?
- One thing to steal. One phrasing or move you're going to use.

## What to watch for

The mechanics are easy to see. The craft is in the small things. Watch for these:

- Silence after a question. Good reps ask, then shut up. Count the seconds.
- Not flinching on price. Watch the rep's face and pace when $70k lands.
- Reframing without arguing. How they turn "we do inbound" or "we can do it internally" into
  a bridge instead of a fight.
- Naming the constraint with a bit of discomfort. The brutal part, delivered warmly.

## After you're cleared

Shadowing never fully ends. Once you're solo, newer reps shadow you, and teaching forces you
to sharpen. Keep a running list of the best moves you steal from teammates. That list becomes
your own playbook.

## Recap

- Three phases: observe, co-pilot, lead with a net. Don't advance until you hit the bar.
- Fill the observation sheet live on every call: trigger, bucket, qualifier, constraint,
  objections, the turn, the close, one thing to steal.
- Watch the craft: silence, not flinching, reframing, the brutal-but-warm constraint.
- Cleared reps become the ones being shadowed. Teaching keeps you sharp.
