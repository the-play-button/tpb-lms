# 🗓️ Your First Two Weeks

Your first two weeks have one job: **get you on real calls, fast, without embarrassing
yourself.** You do that by learning the material cold, rehearsing it out loud until it's
automatic, and then getting on the phone. Here's the plan.

## Your first week, hour by hour

This is the actual schedule. It's built to move you from studying to selling as fast as you
can handle. Monday you're buried in the material; by the end of the week you're doing real
outbound. Every day opens and closes with a coach 1:1.

| Time | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6:00 | Coach 1:1 | Coach 1:1 | Coach 1:1 | Coach 1:1 | Coach 1:1 | Coach 1:1 |
| 6:30 | Coach 1:1 | Study | Study | Roleplay 1:1 | Roleplay 1:1 | Roleplay 1:1 |
| 7:00-8:00 | Study | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal | Outbound | Outbound | Outbound |
| 8:30-9:00 | Study | Roleplay | Roleplay | Outbound | Outbound | Outbound |
| 9:30 | Team training | Team training | Team training | Team training | Team training | Team training |
| 10:00-11:30 | Study, then recording review | Recording review, then roleplay | Recording review, then roleplay | Outbound | Outbound | Outbound |
| 12:00-1:30 | Shadow live calls | Shadow, then roleplay | Shadow, then roleplay | Roleplay | Roleplay | Roleplay |
| 2:00-3:30 | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal | Script rehearsal |

The week moves in one direction: **study-heavy on Monday, calls-heavy by Friday.** By the end
you're spending real hours reaching prospects, not just rehearsing.

The three activities you don't recognize yet:

- **Shadow** = sit in on a real rep's live calls. Hear how it actually sounds, meet the team,
  learn how a call really goes.
- **Recording review** = study call recordings with your script in front of you. Don't just
  listen, sell along with the rep, pause the hard parts, and run them until they're clean.
- **Roleplay tests** = you have to pass three before you go live: **tone**, **recaps**, and
  **objection looping.** Each one you pass, you start taking real calls.

## Two check-ins a day

You meet your coach at the **start and end of every day** in week one. You'll be rough at
first. That's the point. Two check-ins a day is how you stop being rough as fast as possible.

- **Start of day:** they walk you through the agenda, coach you on something specific, and
  answer your questions.
- **End of day:** they review your work, tell you what was good and what wasn't, and give you
  one thing to practice.

## The four phases (and how you pass each)

You move through four phases over two weeks. **You don't advance until you clear the gate.**

| Phase | Days | Your focus | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | 1-2 | Learn it cold. Memorize your scripts. | Recite them from memory. |
| **2** | 2-3 | Deliver it clean. Nail your tone and your recaps. | Perfect delivery + pass the recap test. |
| **3** | 3-4 | Handle objections. Memorize your objection loops. | + pass the objection test. |
| **4** | 4-14 | **Go live.** Take real calls with your coach on standby, then solo. | Meet live-call standards. |

**Phase 1 (Days 1-2):** study the material and memorize the scripts. In parallel, roleplay
and shadow real calls. You pass when you can say the scripts without looking.

**Phase 2 (Days 2-3):** now it has to sound good, not just be memorized. Work your tone and
your recaps (playing back what the prospect said, so they feel heard). Pass = clean delivery
plus a recap test.

**Phase 3 (Days 3-4):** objections. Memorize how you loop each one back to a close. Pass =
everything above, plus an objection test.

**Phase 4 (Days 4-14):** you take **live calls.** Your coach is on standby, then steps back
as you prove you can run one clean. Check-ins drop from two a day to one. You pass when you
hit the live-call standards, and then the training wheels come off.

Nobody runs a perfect call. There's always something to sharpen. That doesn't stop after two
weeks, it's the job. But after two weeks, you're a contributor, not a trainee.
