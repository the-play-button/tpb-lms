# Roleplay Scenarios

You don't get good at selling by reading about it. You get good by saying the words out
loud, badly, until they come out well. This lesson gives you five roleplay scenarios drawn
straight from real Brutal CRM Truth conversations. Run each one with a partner: one plays
the prospect (using the script below), one plays the rep. Then switch. Do each scenario
until you can run it without notes.

**How to run a rep:** hear it fully, don't flinch, reframe, confirm. Anchor value, point at
the guarantee, tie urgency to their trigger. Reviewer scores on the checklist at the end.

## Scenario 1 — The data-fear prospect

**Prospect brief (for your partner to play):** VP Marketing, 120-person B2B manufacturer,
HubSpot. Last campaign delivered at 64%. Interested but visibly nervous about handing over
the contact list.

> **Prospect:** "Honestly, before anything else — I'm not comfortable giving an outside
> company our whole database. What happens to it?"

**Rep goal:** lead with **Your-Data-Stays-Yours** before ROI. Get them from guarded to
curious. Do not confirm any mechanism.

## Scenario 2 — "We can do it internally"

**Prospect brief:** COO, 200 employees, Salesforce. Has a RevOps person. Skeptical this is
worth outside money.

> **Prospect:** "We've got a RevOps analyst. Isn't this just dedupe and some scripts we
> could run ourselves?"

**Rep goal:** reframe tool → outcome + risk. Reference **Zero-Loss Backup** ($5k credit)
and **Rollback**. Land "under four hours of your time, days not weeks, risk on us."

## Scenario 3 — The price flinch

**Prospect brief:** CEO, 80 employees, Pipedrive, ARR ~$9M. Loves the diagnostic, chokes
on $70k.

> **Prospect:** "$70k is a lot for cleaning up a database."

**Rep goal:** don't discount. Re-anchor to ~$700k of value, then hand them **Never-A-Cost**
(they qualify — ARR > $5M). Land "you're structurally incapable of losing money here."

## Scenario 4 — "Not right now"

**Prospect brief:** Head of Sales, 150 employees, HubSpot. Warm, but stalling. Mentioned a
big outbound wave planned for next quarter.

> **Prospect:** "This all sounds good. Let's circle back next quarter."

**Rep goal:** reattach to *their* trigger (the outbound wave), not a fake timer. Land "the
clean has to happen before the send to matter — and it takes 7 days."

## Scenario 5 — The WGE archetype (Custom stack)

**Prospect brief:** COO, 400 employees. Industrial ERP (DECK) + HubSpot that "don't really
talk." No single source of truth. ARR $30M.

> **Prospect:** "Our setup is messy — we've got the ERP and the CRM and they're never in
> sync. I doubt a standard cleanup even applies to us."

**Rep goal:** recognize **Custom CRM stack** in real time. Frame the complexity as a
*bigger, better* engagement (paid Assessment + bridge + segmentation), not a harder sell.
Qualify ARR for Never-A-Cost.

## The reviewer scorecard

After each run, the reviewer scores 0–2 on each:

- [ ] Heard the objection fully before responding (didn't flinch, didn't interrupt).
- [ ] Led with the **right guarantee** for the objection.
- [ ] Anchored **value** with a real number when price came up.
- [ ] Tied urgency to the **prospect's** trigger, not a fake one.
- [ ] Routed the **bucket** correctly (Standard / custom fields / Custom stack).
- [ ] Never revealed or confirmed the delivery **mechanism**.
- [ ] Ended with a clear next step (usually: the free/served Assessment).

Under 10/14, run it again. This is reps, not theory.

## Key takeaways

- Practice out loud with a partner until each scenario runs without notes.
- Five scenarios cover the four objections + the Custom-stack archetype — the conversations
  you'll actually have.
- Score every run on the checklist; under 10/14, do it again.
