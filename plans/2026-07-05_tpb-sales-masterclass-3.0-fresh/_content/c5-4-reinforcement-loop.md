# The Reinforcement Loop

Skills fade without reps. This lesson is the system that keeps you sharp after onboarding —
the weekly rhythm that turns a good first month into a great career. It's short on purpose:
the point isn't to read it, it's to run it every week.

## Weekly coaching

Once a week, you and your coach review **one real call** end to end — ideally one that
stalled. Not to judge it, to dissect it: where did it turn, where did it slip, what would
you do next time? One call, fully understood, beats ten calls glanced at.

Bring a specific question every week. "How do I sell better" is useless. "On call X, the
prospect went cold right after I named the price — what did I miss?" is coachable.

## The call library

Every strong call gets recorded and filed so the team can learn from it. It's organized two
ways so you can find exactly what you need:

- **By moment** — openings, value anchors, objection handles, closes. Struggling with the
  price flinch? Pull five great "price" moments and watch how the best reps handle it.
- **By objection** — data fear, "we'll do it internally," price, "not now." When you keep
  losing on one objection, the library is where you fix it.

Contribute to it. When you nail a call, flag it. Teaching the team sharpens you.

## The feedback loop to product

You're on the front line, so you hear things the product team can't: the objection that
keeps coming up, the trigger nobody anticipated, the phrasing that always lands. Feed it
back. A recurring objection is often a fixable gap in the offer or the collateral — and
you're the one who noticed first.

## Your personal scorecard

Track your own growth over time, not just this week's numbers. Month over month: are your
conversion rates climbing? Are the objections that used to stump you now automatic? Are you
closing bigger, more complex deals (the Custom-stack archetype) without help? That
trajectory — not any single call — is the real measure.

## The loop, in one picture

> **Sell → record → review one call weekly → steal from the library → feed the gap back →
> repeat.**

That's it. Run it every week and you compound. Skip it and you plateau. The reps who win
aren't the ones with the most talent — they're the ones who never stop running the loop.

## Key takeaways

- **Weekly coaching** on one real call, with a specific question — depth beats volume.
- The **call library** (by moment, by objection) is where you fix a weakness; contribute to
  it.
- **Feed the front-line signal back** to product — recurring objections are fixable gaps.
- Track your **trajectory** month over month. Run the loop every week — that's what
  compounds.
