# The Reinforcement Loop

Skills fade without reps. This lesson is the system that keeps you sharp after onboarding,
the weekly rhythm that turns a good first month into a good career. It's short on purpose.
The point isn't to read it, it's to run it every week.

## Weekly coaching

Once a week, you and your coach go through one real call end to end, ideally one that
stalled. Not to judge it, to take it apart. Where did it turn, where did it slip, what would
you do next time? One call, fully understood, beats ten calls glanced at.

Bring a specific question every week. "How do I sell better" is useless. "On call X, the
prospect went cold right after I named the price, what did I miss?" is something a coach can
actually work with.

## The call library

Every strong call gets recorded and filed so the team can learn from it. It's organized two
ways so you can find what you need.

By moment: openings, value anchors, objection handles, closes. Struggling with the price
flinch? Pull five great "price" moments and watch how the best reps handle it.

By objection: data fear, "we'll do it internally," price, "not now." When you keep losing on
one objection, the library is where you fix it.

Contribute to it. When you nail a call, flag it. Teaching the team sharpens you too.

## Feed the signal back to product

You're on the front line, so you hear things the product team can't: the objection that keeps
coming up, the trigger nobody anticipated, the phrasing that always lands. Feed it back. A
recurring objection is often a fixable gap in the offer or the collateral, and you're the one
who noticed first.

## Track your own trajectory

Track your growth over time, not just this week's numbers. Month over month, are your
conversion rates climbing? Are the objections that used to stump you now automatic? Are you
closing bigger, more complex deals, like the Custom-stack archetype, without help? That
trajectory is the real measure, not any single call.

## The loop

Sell, record, review one call a week, steal from the library, feed the gap back, repeat.

That's it. Run it every week and you compound. Skip it and you plateau. The reps who win
aren't the ones with the most talent, they're the ones who never stop running the loop.

## Recap

- Weekly coaching on one real call, with a specific question. Depth beats volume.
- The call library, by moment and by objection, is where you fix a weakness. Contribute to
  it.
- Feed the front-line signal back to product. Recurring objections are fixable gaps.
- Track your trajectory month over month. Run the loop every week. That's what compounds.
