# 📈 The Reinforcement Loop

**Run this every week and you compound. Skip it and you plateau.**

> Sell → record → review one call → steal from the library → feed the gap back → repeat.

## Weekly coaching

Once a week, you and your coach take **one real call apart,** ideally one that stalled. Where
did it turn, where did it slip, what would you do next time. **Bring a specific question.** Not
"how do I sell better," but "on this call the prospect went cold right after I named the
price, what did I miss?"

## The call library

Every strong call gets filed two ways: **by moment** (openings, value anchors, objection
handles, closes) and **by objection** (data fear, "we'll do it internally," price, "not
now"). Losing on the price flinch? Pull five great price moments and watch how the best reps
do it. When you nail a call, flag it. **Teaching the team sharpens you.**

## Feed the signal back

You're on the front line, so you hear what the product team can't: the objection that keeps
coming up, the trigger nobody expected, the phrasing that always lands. **A recurring
objection is usually a fixable gap in the offer, and you noticed first. Say something.**

## Track your trajectory

Month over month: are your conversion rates climbing? Are the objections that used to stump
you now automatic? Are you closing bigger, messier deals without help? **That's the real
measure, not any single call.**
