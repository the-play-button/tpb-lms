# 🗺️ How Companies Actually Go To Market

**Every company on earth has salespeople. No exceptions.** That one fact is why our offer
is universal, and it's the thing most people get wrong.

The common picture is "marketing generates leads, then sales closes them." It feels like the
default. **It isn't.** You only notice the companies that are loud online. The quiet majority
don't run content engines or big ad budgets, and every one of them still has reps. So the
real default of the market is **sales-led.** Not a stance we're defending, just what you see
when you look at the whole market instead of the loud slice of it.

## When a prospect pushes back

Some prospects will tell you they're "mostly inbound" or "marketing-driven." **Don't
argue.** Connect their world to ours:

> "Perfect, and inbound only pays off if the base those leads land in is clean. Right now,
> how much of your marketing spend is landing on duplicate, stale, or dead records?"

You just turned their motion into a reason to talk to us, without a fight.

## The four ways companies grow

Worth knowing so nothing throws you:

- **Sales-led:** reps, one to one. Universal.
- **Marketing-led:** ads and content. Rarer than people think.
- **Partner-led:** resellers and affiliates.
- **Product-led:** the product sells itself, mostly SaaS.

We sell **sales-led plus the clean data underneath it,** because that need is universal. We
never wave away the other three. A clean base makes all of them work better, and that's
always your bridge.
