# How Companies Actually Go To Market

To sell well, you need an accurate map of how businesses grow — not the LinkedIn version.
This lesson gives you that map and kills one very common, very expensive myth.

## The four go-to-market paths

There are four ways a company drives growth. Know all four so you can place any prospect
instantly:

| Path | How it moves | Reality in the market |
|---|---|---|
| **Sales-led** | Human reps, one-to-one | **Universal** — *every* company has reps. No exceptions. |
| **Marketing-led** | Paid ads + organic content + marketplaces (no ABM) | **Rarer than you think** — few companies actually publish or build a brand. |
| **Partner-led** | Resellers, affiliates, co-marketing | Depends on the sector. |
| **Product-led** | The product sells itself (mostly SaaS) | Mostly SaaS. |

## The myth that will cost you deals

The "classic funnel" — *marketing generates leads, then sales closes them* — feels like
the default. **It isn't.** That picture is a survivorship bias: you only see the companies
loud online. The quiet majority don't run content engines or big paid budgets — but every
single one of them has salespeople.

So the real default of the market is **sales-led**. That's not an opinion or a stance we're
defending — it's just what's true when you look at the whole market instead of the visible
slice of it.

## What this means for us

Two things follow, and they matter for how you talk to prospects:

1. **What we sell is sales-led + the data foundation under it.** Our offer makes the sales
   motion work — a clean base, a pipeline that produces, reps who trust their numbers.
   That's the universal need, so it's what we lead with.

2. **Choosing what to *sell* is not the same as dismissing the other paths.** We ourselves
   use marketing, partners, and product internally. A prospect may run any of the four. You
   never wave away their marketing or their product — you meet them where they are and show
   how a clean, trustworthy base makes *whatever* they're doing work better. Sales-led is
   our wedge, not a religion.

**Concrete application:** when a prospect says *"we're mostly inbound / marketing-driven,"*
you don't argue. You say: *"Perfect — and inbound only pays off if the base those leads
land in is clean. Right now, how much of your marketing spend is landing on duplicate,
stale, or dead records?"* You just connected their world to ours without a fight.

## Key takeaways

- Four GTM paths: **sales-led, marketing-led, partner-led, product-led.** Know all four to
  place any prospect.
- **Sales-led is the market default** — every company has reps. "Marketing → sales" as the
  norm is survivorship bias.
- We **sell** sales-led + the data foundation, because it's the universal need — but we
  never dismiss the other paths.
- Meet prospects where they are; show how a clean base makes *their* motion work better.
