# How Companies Actually Go To Market

To sell well you need an accurate picture of how businesses grow. Not the LinkedIn version,
the real one. This lesson gives you that picture and kills a common, expensive myth.

## The four go-to-market paths

There are four ways a company drives growth. Know all four so you can place any prospect
fast.

| Path | How it moves | Reality in the market |
|---|---|---|
| Sales-led | Human reps, one to one | Universal. Every company has reps. No exceptions. |
| Marketing-led | Paid ads, organic content, marketplaces (no ABM) | Rarer than you'd think. Few companies actually publish or build a brand. |
| Partner-led | Resellers, affiliates, co-marketing | Depends on the sector. |
| Product-led | The product sells itself | Mostly SaaS. |

## The myth that costs you deals

The "classic funnel", where marketing generates leads and then sales closes them, feels
like the default. It isn't. That picture is survivorship bias. You only notice the companies
that are loud online. The quiet majority don't run content engines or big paid budgets, and
every one of them still has salespeople.

So the real default of the market is sales-led. That's not an opinion or a stance we're
defending. It's just what you see when you look at the whole market instead of the visible
slice of it.

## What this means for us

Two things follow, and both matter for how you talk to prospects.

First, what we sell is sales-led plus the data foundation under it. Our offer makes the
sales motion work: a clean base, a pipeline that produces, reps who trust their numbers.
That need is universal, so it's what we lead with.

Second, choosing what to sell is not the same as dismissing the other paths. We use
marketing, partners, and product ourselves. A prospect might run any of the four. You never
wave away their marketing or their product. You meet them where they are and show how a
clean, trustworthy base makes whatever they're already doing work better.

Here's how that sounds in practice. When a prospect says "we're mostly inbound", you don't
argue. You say: "Perfect, and inbound only pays off if the base those leads land in is
clean. Right now, how much of your marketing spend is landing on duplicate, stale, or dead
records?" You just connected their world to ours without a fight.

## Recap

- Four GTM paths: sales-led, marketing-led, partner-led, product-led. Know all four so you
  can place any prospect.
- Sales-led is the market default. Every company has reps. "Marketing then sales" as the
  norm is survivorship bias.
- We sell sales-led plus the data foundation because it's the universal need. We never
  dismiss the other paths.
- Meet prospects where they are and show how a clean base makes their motion work better.
