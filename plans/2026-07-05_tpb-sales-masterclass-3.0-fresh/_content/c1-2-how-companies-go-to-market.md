# How Companies Actually Go To Market

Every company on earth has salespeople. No exceptions. That one fact is why our offer is
universal, and it's the thing most people get wrong.

The common picture is "marketing generates leads, then sales closes them." It feels like
the default. It isn't. You only notice the companies that are loud online. The quiet
majority don't run content engines or big ad budgets, and every one of them still has reps.
So the real default of the market is sales-led. That's not a stance we're defending, it's
just what you see when you look at the whole market instead of the loud slice of it.

## What to do when a prospect pushes back

Some prospects will tell you they're "mostly inbound" or "marketing-driven." Don't argue.
Connect their world to ours:

> "Perfect, and inbound only pays off if the base those leads land in is clean. Right now,
> how much of your marketing spend is landing on duplicate, stale, or dead records?"

You just turned their motion into a reason to talk to us, without a fight.

## The four ways companies grow

Worth knowing so nothing throws you: sales-led (reps, universal), marketing-led (ads and
content, rarer than people think), partner-led (resellers and affiliates), and product-led
(the product sells itself, mostly SaaS). We sell sales-led plus the clean data underneath
it, because that need is universal. We never wave away the other three. A clean base makes
all of them work better, and that's always your bridge.
