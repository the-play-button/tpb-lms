# Objection Handling For This Offer

You learned the general framework in the Sales Conversation course: hear it fully, don't
flinch, reframe, confirm. This lesson gives you the four objections you'll actually hear on
The Brutal CRM Truth, and how to handle each. Most of them are already handled by the offer
itself, if you know where to point.

## Objection 1: "What are you going to do with our data?"

This is the number one objection in mid-market B2B, and it's usually unspoken. Until it's
answered, nothing else matters. Not ROI, not price. Handle it before they even ask, and lead
with the guarantee:

> "Let me get the most important thing out of the way first, because everyone thinks it and
> few people say it. Your data stays 100% yours. We're a service provider, not a data buyer.
> Your CRM data is never sold, never shared with a competitor, never used to train someone's
> AI. It's in the contract. No resale, no competitor-sharing, no third-party model training.
> You can even ask for an audit log to see exactly what we touched."

That's the Your-Data-Stays-Yours Guarantee, and it's unconditional. Every configuration,
every tier. Say it early and say it plainly. It's the price of entry to a yes.

## Objection 2: "We could do this internally. Isn't it just software?"

Don't get defensive, and don't confirm the mechanism. Move from tool to outcome and risk:

> "You could, and some teams do. The question isn't whether it's possible, it's what it
> costs you. An operator's weeks, the risk of breaking your reporting halfway through, and
> it still isn't maintained six months later. We do it in days, you spend under four hours,
> and if we change a single record without your approval we restore it and hand you $5,000 in
> credit. You're not buying a script, you're buying the outcome, guaranteed, with none of the
> risk on you."

That last line points at the Zero-Loss Backup Guarantee ($5k credit if we touch a record
without their approval) and the 30-Day One-Click Rollback (full reversal, no questions,
within 30 days). Two more reasons the risk sits with us, not them.

## Objection 3: "It's expensive."

Don't discount. Go back to the value math from the last lesson, then hand them the strongest
guarantee:

> "Compared to what? A dirty CRM is conservatively costing you $700k a year in dead pipeline,
> blown deliverability, and rep hours. This is $70k. And here's the part that makes the price
> a non-issue. If you're above $5M ARR, this is Never-A-Cost. Every quarter we show you the
> ROI dashboard, and if you haven't recouped what you paid, we keep working for free until
> you have. You can't lose money on this."

For clients under $5M ARR, with no Never-A-Cost, fall back to Win-Your-Money-Back. If the
objective scorecard targets aren't hit (STRONG duplicates down 50%, lifecycle accuracy up
30%), they get a 100% cash refund. Either way the risk is with us.

## Objection 4: "Not right now."

"Not now" is almost always "no trigger surfaced" or "no urgency." Go back to their calendar,
not a fake timer:

> "Fair enough. I'd just tie it to your own deadline, not mine. You mentioned a campaign
> going out next quarter. If the base is 30% dead when that sends, you burn sender reputation
> for 90 days. The clean has to happen before the send to matter, and for a standard setup
> we're done in 7 days. So the real question is, when's the next thing that depends on this
> data being right?"

We never impose a countdown. The urgency is their event: the campaign, the quarter close,
the new VP's 90-day review, the migration cutover. Anchor to it.

## The pattern

Notice what's going on. On this offer the objections are mostly pre-handled by the
guarantees. Your job isn't to be clever, it's to know the four objections cold and point at
the guarantee that dissolves each one. Data fear goes to Your-Data-Stays-Yours. Risk goes to
Zero-Loss plus Rollback. Price goes to Never-A-Cost, or WYMB under $5M. Timing goes to their
trigger.

## Recap

- Data ownership is the gating fear. Lead with the unconditional guarantee before they ask.
- "We'll do it internally" goes to outcome and risk. You carry the risk, they don't.
- Price goes back to about $700k of value, then Never-A-Cost (or WYMB under $5M). They can't
  lose money.
- "Not now" reattaches to their deadline. Never invent a timer.
