# Objection Handling For This Offer

Four objections come up, and each one is already killed by a guarantee. Your job is to know
which guarantee answers which, and reach for it fast.

## "What are you going to do with our data?"

Number one objection, usually unspoken. Until it's answered, nothing else matters. Handle it
before they ask:

> "Let me get the biggest thing out of the way, because everyone thinks it and few say it.
> Your data stays 100% yours. We're a service provider, not a data buyer. Never sold, never
> shared with a competitor, never used to train anyone's AI. It's in the contract, and you
> can ask for an audit log of exactly what we touched."

That's the Your-Data-Stays-Yours guarantee. Unconditional. Say it early.

## "We could do this internally. Isn't it just software?"

Don't get defensive, and don't confirm the mechanism. Move to outcome and risk:

> "You could, and some teams do. The question is what it costs you: an operator's weeks, the
> risk of breaking your reporting halfway through, and it still isn't maintained six months
> later. We do it in days, you spend under four hours, and if we change one record without
> your approval we restore it and hand you $5,000. You're buying the outcome, guaranteed,
> with none of the risk on you."

That's Zero-Loss Backup ($5k credit) plus the 30-day rollback.

## "It's expensive."

Don't discount. Re-anchor and hand them the guarantee:

> "Compared to what? A dirty CRM is costing you $700k a year, conservatively. This is $70k.
> And if you're above $5M ARR, this is Never-A-Cost: every quarter we show you the ROI, and
> if you haven't recouped what you paid, we work for free until you have. You can't lose
> money on this."

Under $5M ARR, fall back to money-back: miss the scorecard targets (duplicates down 50%,
lifecycle up 30%) and it's a full cash refund.

## "Not right now."

Almost always means no urgency surfaced. Go to their calendar, not a fake timer:

> "Fair. I'd tie it to your deadline, not mine. You mentioned a campaign next quarter. If the
> base is 30% dead when it sends, you burn sender reputation for 90 days. The clean has to
> happen before the send, and it takes 7 days. So when's the next thing that depends on this
> data being right?"
