# Objection Handling For This Offer

You learned the general framework in the Sales Conversation course: hear it fully, don't
flinch, reframe, confirm. This lesson gives you the **four objections you will actually
hear** on The Brutal CRM Truth, and the exact way to handle each — most of them are
already handled by the offer itself, if you know how to point at it.

## Objection 1 — "What are you going to do with our data?" (the gating fear)

This is the **number one** objection in mid-market B2B, and it's usually unspoken. Until
it's answered, nothing else matters — not ROI, not price. Handle it before they even ask,
and lead with the guarantee:

> *"Let me get the most important thing out of the way first, because everyone thinks it
> and few people say it: your data stays 100% yours. We're a service provider, not a data
> buyer. Your CRM data is never sold, never shared with a competitor, never used to train
> someone's AI. It's written into the contract — no resale, no competitor-sharing, no
> third-party model training. You can even ask for an audit log to see exactly what we
> touched."*

This is the **Your-Data-Stays-Yours Guarantee**, and it's unconditional — every
configuration, every tier. Say it early and say it plainly. It's the price of entry to a
yes.

## Objection 2 — "We could do this internally / isn't this just software?"

Never get defensive, and never confirm the mechanism. Reframe from *tool* to *outcome +
risk*:

> *"You absolutely could — and some teams do. The question isn't whether it's possible,
> it's what it costs you: an operator's weeks, the risk of breaking your reporting
> mid-way, and it still isn't maintained six months later. We do it in days, you spend
> under four hours, and if we alter a single record without your approval we restore it
> and hand you $5,000 in credit. You're not buying a script — you're buying the outcome,
> guaranteed, with none of the risk on you."*

That last line references the **Zero-Loss Backup Guarantee** ($5k credit if we touch a
record without their swipe approval) and the **30-Day One-Click Rollback** (full reversal,
no questions, within 30 days). Two more reasons the risk is on us, not them.

## Objection 3 — "It's expensive."

Don't discount. Re-anchor to the value math (from the Value lesson) and hand them the
strongest guarantee:

> *"Compared to what? A dirty CRM is conservatively costing you $700k a year in dead
> pipeline, blown deliverability, and rep hours. This is $70k. And here's the part that
> should make the price a non-issue: if you're above $5M ARR, this is Never-A-Cost — every
> quarter we show you the ROI dashboard, and if you haven't recouped what you paid, we keep
> working for free until you have. You are structurally incapable of losing money here."*

For clients under $5M ARR (no Never-A-Cost), fall back to **Win-Your-Money-Back**: if the
objective scorecard targets aren't hit (dedupe -50%, lifecycle accuracy +30%), they get a
**100% cash refund**. Either way, the risk sits with us.

## Objection 4 — "Not right now."

"Not now" is almost always "no trigger surfaced" or "no urgency." Go back to *their*
calendar, not a fake timer:

> *"Totally fair — I'd just tie it to your own deadline, not mine. You mentioned a campaign
> going out next quarter. If the base is 30% dead when that sends, you burn sender
> reputation for 90 days. The clean has to happen *before* the send to matter — and for a
> standard setup we're done in 7 days. So the real question is: when's the next thing that
> depends on this data being right?"*

We never impose a countdown. The urgency is **their** event — the campaign, the quarter
close, the new VP's 90-day review, the migration cutover. Anchor to it.

## The mindset

Notice the pattern: on this offer, **the objections are pre-handled by the guarantees.**
Your job isn't to be clever — it's to know the four objections cold and point at the exact
guarantee that dissolves each one. Data fear → Your-Data-Stays-Yours. Risk → Zero-Loss +
Rollback. Price → Never-A-Cost / WYMB. Timing → their trigger.

## Key takeaways

- **Data ownership** is the gating fear — lead with the unconditional guarantee before
  they ask.
- **"We'll do it internally"** → reframe to outcome + risk; you carry the risk, they don't.
- **Price** → re-anchor to ~$700k of value, then hand them **Never-A-Cost** (or WYMB under
  $5M). They can't lose money.
- **"Not now"** → reattach to *their* deadline; never invent a timer.
