# 💰 The Value: Why It Pays For Itself

**A dirty CRM costs a mid-market company about $700k a year, conservatively. You sell the fix
for $70k.** Carry that ratio in your head and you stop flinching on price.

**Where the $700k comes from.** Memorize the three sources and the one-liner for each:

| Source | 12-month range | Say |
|---|---|---|
| **Dormant pipeline reactivated** | $80k to $400k | "You've got 1.5k to 2.5k dormant contacts. Wake a fraction and that's 7 to 25 deals at your average size." |
| **Sender reputation preserved** | $150k to $600k | "One bad blast costs you a quarter of email. That's $100k to $300k of pipeline gone for 90 days." |
| **Sales ops efficiency** | $200k to $1.5M | "Reps lose about 27% of their time to bad data. Dirty CRM taxes 12% to 25% of ARR." |

**Anchor it live:**

> "Let's be conservative and say this is worth $700k to you this year. The engagement is $70k.
> If I'm even in the right zip code, this isn't a cost, it's the best-returning line on your
> budget. And it's guaranteed to pay for itself."

That last line **isn't a slogan.** For clients above $5M ARR, the **Never-A-Cost Guarantee**
means if a quarterly check shows they haven't recouped what they paid, we keep working for
free until they have. That's why you can say "pays for itself" and mean it.

And when the diagnostic is free, **that raises value, it doesn't cheapen it.** A free, instant
read tells them you're sure. Never present it like an apology.
