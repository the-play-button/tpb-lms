# The Value: Why It Pays For Itself

To sell a $70k engagement with a straight face, you have to believe it's a bargain, and you
have to show the math in about sixty seconds. This lesson gives you both.

## The value equation you're moving

Every offer's perceived value comes down to one equation from Hormozi:

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

Our full done-for-you engagement is built to win on all four at once.

| Lever | Before (a DemandTools, Validity, or boutique consultant) | After (TPB, full DFY) |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | "clean CRM," a utility, low status | Run the business on truth, know your real constraint, stay clean over time |
| Perceived Likelihood | "expensive promise, no proof" | Four written guarantees, a public scorecard method, 62 projects behind us |
| Time Delay | 1 to 3 months, audit and cleaning done separately | First scorecard in about a day, diagnostic in 7 to 10 days, cleaning in 7 to 14 |
| Effort & Sacrifice | an internal operator plus weeks of setup | Under 4 hours of client time, we do the rest |

You don't need the exact score. You need the instinct. We make the outcome bigger and more
certain, and we make it faster and nearly effortless. That's a real jump in value, not a 10%
improvement.

## The dollars

When a prospect hears "$70k," your job is to make that number feel small next to what a
dirty CRM is costing them right now. Three sources, over 12 months.

| Where the money is | 12-month range | The one-liner |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline recovery, dormant deals reactivated | $80k to $400k | "You've got 1.5k to 2.5k dormant contacts. Wake a fraction of them and that's 7 to 25 deals at your average size." |
| Sender reputation preserved | $150k to $600k | "One bad blast can cost you a full quarter of email. That's $100k to $300k of pipeline you run through email, gone for 90 days." |
| Sales ops efficiency | $200k to $1.5M | "Reps lose about 27% of their time to bad data, per Validity and ZoomInfo. Dirty CRM quietly taxes 12% to 25% of ARR." |

A conservative central number is about $715k of value. The honest central number is closer
to $1.2M. Against a $70k configuration, you're selling a dollar for a dime, and you can say
so.

Here's how you anchor the price:

> "Let's be conservative and say this is worth $700k to you over the year. Dormant pipeline,
> protected deliverability, hours back for your reps. The engagement is $70k. If I'm even in
> the right zip code, this isn't a cost, it's the best-returning line item on your budget.
> And it's guaranteed to pay for itself."

## "Pays for itself" is a guarantee, not a slogan

The headline promise, that it pays for itself or we keep working until it does, is backed by
a real mechanism you'll cover in the objections and closing lessons: the Never-A-Cost
Guarantee. For qualified clients (ARR above $5M, engagement $25k or more), if a quarterly
checkpoint shows they haven't recouped what they paid, we keep working for free until they
have. That's why you can say "pays for itself" and mean it.

## Free is a lever, not a giveaway

For Standard CRM clients the Assessment is free, and that actually raises value instead of
cheapening it. A free, instant diagnostic maxes out two levers at once. Perceived
Likelihood, because they'll show me first so they must be sure. And Time Delay, because I
see something today. Never present the free Assessment like an apology. Present it as proof
of confidence.

## Recap

- You move four levers at once: bigger and more certain outcome, faster and near-zero
  effort. That's a jump, not an upgrade.
- Memorize the three dollar sources: pipeline ($80k to $400k), deliverability ($150k to
  $600k), sales ops ($200k to $1.5M). Conservative floor about $715k.
- Anchor the price against that number and $70k feels like a rounding error.
- "Pays for itself" is real. It's the Never-A-Cost Guarantee, not marketing.
