# The Value — Why It Pays For Itself

To sell a $70k engagement with confidence, you have to *believe* it's a bargain — and be
able to show the math in sixty seconds. This lesson gives you the numbers and the mental
model so you never flinch on price again.

## The value equation you're moving

Every offer's perceived value comes down to Hormozi's equation:

> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Our full done-for-you engagement is engineered to win on all four levers at once:

| Lever | Before (a DemandTools / Validity / boutique consultant) | After (TPB, full DFY) |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** | "clean CRM" — a utility, low status | "run the business on truth, know your real constraint, and stay clean over time" |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | "expensive promise, no proof" | four written guarantees + a public scorecard method + 62 battle-tested projects |
| **Time Delay** | 1–3 months, audit and cleaning done separately | first scorecard in ~24h, diagnostic in 7–10 days, cleaning in 7–14 |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | an internal operator + weeks of setup | under 4 hours of client time, gamified approval UX, we do the rest |

You don't need the exact score. You need the instinct: **we make the outcome bigger and
more certain, and we make it faster and nearly effortless.** That is a step-change in
value, not a 10% improvement.

## The dollars (honest baseline — use these live)

When a prospect hears "$70k," your job is to make that number feel small next to what a
dirty CRM is costing them *right now*. Three sources, over 12 months:

| Where the money is | 12-month range | The one-liner |
|---|---|---|
| **Pipeline recovery** — dormant deals reactivated | **$80–400k** | "You have 1.5–2.5k dormant contacts. Wake a fraction of them and that's 7–25 deals at your average size." |
| **Sender reputation preserved** | **$150–600k** | "One bad blast can cost you a full quarter of email — that's $100–300k of pipeline you run through email, gone for 90 days." |
| **Sales ops efficiency** | **$200–1,500k** | "Reps lose ~27% of their time to bad data (Validity/ZoomInfo). Dirty CRM quietly taxes 12–25% of ARR." |

**Central honest number: ~$1.2M of value.** Conservative floor: **~$715k.** Against a
$70k configuration, you are selling a dollar for a dime — and you can say so.

**Concrete script — anchoring price:**

> *"Let's be conservative and say this is worth $700k to you over the year — dormant
> pipeline, protected deliverability, hours back for your reps. The engagement is $70k.
> If I'm even in the right zip code, this isn't a cost, it's the best-returning line item
> on your budget. And it's guaranteed to pay for itself."*

## "Pays for itself" is not a slogan — it's a guarantee

The headline promise ("it pays for itself, or we keep working until it does") is backed by
a real mechanism you'll cover in the objections and closing lessons: the **Never-A-Cost
Guarantee**. For qualified clients (ARR above $5M, engagement $25k+), if a quarterly
checkpoint shows they haven't recouped what they paid, we keep working — free — until they
have. That's why you can say "pays for itself" and mean it.

## Free is a lever, not a giveaway

For Standard CRM clients, the Assessment is free — and counterintuitively that **raises**
value, it doesn't cheapen it. A free, instant diagnostic maxes out two levers at once:
Perceived Likelihood ("they'll show me first, they must be sure") and Time Delay ("I see
something today"). Never present the free Assessment apologetically. Present it as proof
of confidence.

## Key takeaways

- You move four levers at once: **bigger + more certain outcome, faster + near-zero
  effort.** That's a step-change, not an upgrade.
- Memorize the three dollar sources: **pipeline ($80–400k), deliverability ($150–600k),
  sales ops ($200–1,500k).** Conservative floor ~$715k, central ~$1.2M.
- Anchor price against that number and the $70k feels like a rounding error.
- "Pays for itself" is real — it's the **Never-A-Cost Guarantee**, not marketing.
