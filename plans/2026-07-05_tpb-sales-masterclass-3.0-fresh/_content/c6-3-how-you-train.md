# 🎯 How You Train (Every Day)

You never stop training. The reps who win aren't the most talented, they're the ones who run
the drills every single day. Talent gets you a good week. Reps get you a good year. Here's how
you drill.

## Memorize your script cold

You can't sound natural reading. You sound natural when the words are so automatic you forget
they're a script. Get there with the blackout method.

1. **Read** your script out loud, looking at it.
2. **Cover one line**, say the whole thing, uncover to check.
3. **Cover two lines.** Repeat.
4. Keep covering more until **the page is blank and you can still say it clean.**

**Gate:** you can recite it start to finish with nothing in front of you. Do this for your
opener, your discovery questions, your recaps, and every objection loop.

## Roleplay the five-step way

Reading a script and running one on a live human are different skills. Roleplay is where you
build the second one. Run every drill in five steps.

| Step | What you do |
|---|---|
| **1. Frame it** | Say what you're drilling and what "good" sounds like. |
| **2. Model it** | Watch someone good run it once. Hear the tone, the pace, the pauses. |
| **3. Copy it** | You run it, matching what you just heard. |
| **4. Practice it** | Run it again and again. Your partner throws curveballs. |
| **5. Recap it** | Name what you did well and the one thing to fix next time. |

**Do each drill until you can run it without notes and without freezing.** That's the bar, not
"I did it once."

## Watch real calls, actively

Recordings are your game tape. But watching isn't sitting back with popcorn. Watch with a job.

- **Before:** pick one thing to study (how they open, how they handle a price objection).
- **During:** pause when something works. Say out loud what they did and why it landed.
- **After:** find the same moment in one of your own calls. What would you change now?

**Say:** "What did they do in the first thirty seconds that I don't?" Then go steal it.

## Your training week

Training isn't a phase you finish. It's a weekly rhythm you keep for as long as you sell.

| Cadence | What happens |
|---|---|
| **Daily** | 30 minutes of roleplay or script drills before calls start. |
| **Weekly** | Review one of your own recorded calls with your coach, start to finish. |
| **Weekly** | One-on-one: rate your own week 1 to 10, pick the **one skill** you drill next week. |

**Pick one skill, not five.** The rep who gets 10% better at one thing every week is
unrecognizable in three months. The rep who tries to fix everything fixes nothing.
