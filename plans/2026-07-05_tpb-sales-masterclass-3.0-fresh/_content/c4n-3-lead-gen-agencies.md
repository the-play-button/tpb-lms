# 🎯 ICP: Lead-Generation Agencies

**Who they are:** close cousins of SDR agencies, but they **stop earlier in the funnel.** Where
an SDR agency runs the outreach and books meetings, a lead-gen agency mostly does the front
end: **sourcing, enrichment, and list building**, then hands the list off. Think of them as
the sourcing-and-data specialists of the GTM stack.

## Why they're a distinct test

They share a lot with SDR agencies (same stack, same clients, same margin pressure), so much of
the SDR module's language and pain applies. Read that module first, then layer on the
differences below.

## The delicate part: know it, don't lead with it

Our capability lets **anyone** do sourcing, enrichment, and pushing a clean list into a
sequencer without an agency. That's exactly what a lead-gen agency sells. So this niche is
sensitive: taken to its logical end, we can do most of their core work.

The honest, respectful position: **AI won't replace their expertise.** We do roughly 80% of
the mechanical work and leave them the 20% that's judgment, relationships, and strategy, which
cuts their operating cost sharply. You lead with "we make your delivery cheaper and cleaner,"
not with "we replace you." Hold the deeper ambition quietly; on a call, the message is
partnership, not disruption.

## Their words, pain, and timing

Largely the same as SDR agencies (see that module), with the weight shifted toward the front of
the funnel: **data quality, list accuracy, enrichment cost, deliverability, sourcing speed.**
Their triggers look similar too (scaling, data-quality crises, client reporting demands).
