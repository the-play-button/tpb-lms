# ⚙️ ICP: GTM Operators

**Who they are:** the people **inside a company** who own the go-to-market stack and its data,
rather than an agency running it for clients. Titles like RevOps, Sales Ops, Head of Growth, or
a hands-on VP Sales. This is a deliberately **broader** target than agencies: instead of firms
that sell outbound as a service, it's every company running its own outbound and CRM.

## Why they're on the list

Agencies are a sharp, concentrated beachhead, but they're a small pool. GTM operators are the
much larger market: any mid-market B2B company with a messy CRM and a team that runs outreach.
When we broaden from agencies, this is where we go. That includes the secondary targets we've
always noted: **SaaS B2B mid-market, consulting firms, and industrial manufacturers.**

## How they differ from agencies

- **They own the outcome, not a client's outcome.** No SOW, no client audit, no white-label
  pressure. The pain is internal: their own pipeline, their own reporting to leadership.
- **The buyer is the operator or the VP**, not an agency owner protecting margin per seat.
- **Slower, cleaner decisions** in bigger companies, but bigger and stickier when they land.

## Their words, pain, and timing

The GTM-ecosystem module is the backbone here: they live in the CRM / dialer / sequencer /
enrichment stack and feel the data rot directly. Their triggers lean toward: **a new VP Sales
or RevOps in their first 60 days** (wants quick wins and data they trust), a **paused outbound
launch** blocked on data quality, a **post-merger CRM reconciliation**, and **deliverability
dropping** before a campaign.
