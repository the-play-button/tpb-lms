# 🌐 The GTM Ecosystem

**Every niche we target lives inside the same tool stack: the go-to-market (GTM) stack.** If
you know who does what in that stack, you speak your buyer's language and you know exactly
where we fit. Learn this once and it pays off in every niche.

## The four ways anyone acquires customers

Underneath every GTM stack are four acquisition motions. A buyer is always doing some mix of
these:

- **Outreach** (cold): you reach out directly, one to one.
- **Network** (warm): you leverage relationships you already have.
- **Content**: you publish, one to many.
- **Ads**: you pay a platform to reach many.

On top of those, people find **ambassadors** who acquire for them: happy customers who refer,
employees who do the motions, external agencies who run one of them, and partners or
influencers on commission. Our world is **"many one-to-one"**: selling at scale, not one-to-
many broadcasting and not one-to-many spamming.

## The tool stack, layer by layer

Your buyers stitch together best-of-breed tools. Know the categories and the leaders:

| Layer | What it does | Common tools |
|---|---|---|
| **CRM** | System of record for contacts, companies, deals | HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive |
| **Dialer** | Powers the calls | Aircall, plus dialers inside Outreach/SalesLoft |
| **Prospecting / data** | Finds leads and contact info | Apollo, Lusha, ZoomInfo |
| **Enrichment / orchestration** | Enriches and automates across tools | Clay |
| **Sequencers / cadence** | Runs multi-touch email and LinkedIn | Lemlist, Outreach, SalesLoft, Instantly |
| **Conversation intelligence** | Records and analyzes calls | Gong, Chorus, Fireflies |

**The rule to remember:** these tools don't talk to each other well, and the data flowing
between them rots fast. That gap is our home. Everything above only works if the underlying
data is clean, which is exactly what we sell.

## Where we sit

We're the **clean-data foundation** under this stack. We don't replace the CRM, the dialer,
the sequencer, or the prospecting tools. We make the data they all depend on trustworthy, so
every layer above performs. When a buyer says "we already have HubSpot and Apollo and Lemlist,"
that's not a competitor, that's a stack that's leaking value because the data underneath is
dirty.
